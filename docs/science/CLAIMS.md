# CLAIMS — The User-Facing Factual-Claims Register

This is the **load-bearing register** of Hifz Companion's science foundation. It indexes **every user-facing factual claim the app makes** — in the "The science we follow" screen ([`11-the-in-app-science-screen.md`](11-the-in-app-science-screen.md)), in onboarding and recite-flow copy, in notification text, in the methodology and progress screens, and in any tooltip or help string that asserts something about memory, spaced repetition, traditional ḥifẓ methodology, or motivation. The rule is absolute (blueprint §2; [`README.md`](README.md) science value 1): **if a statement is not registered here with at least one verified source and an evidence grade, the app must not state it.** The chain is one-directional — *verified primary source → graded claim in a synthesis doc → this register → engine rule + on-screen copy → the science screen* — so a wrong entry here ships straight to a ḥāfiẓ. Each entry therefore carries the claim *as the app states it*, the *value/rule the engine actually uses*, the *inline-cited source(s)*, the *grade*, the *app surface* where it appears, and *caveats* — including, where relevant, the honest note that the app simplifies the literature ("the app uses X; the research shows X–Y").

The full evidence behind each row lives in the synthesis docs (`01`–`11`) and the deep dossiers in [`research/`](research/); the deduplicated, fully-formatted master bibliography is in [`REFERENCES.md`](REFERENCES.md). This file is the single point of authorship the in-app science screen renders.

**Scope & neutrality (binding on every religious/methodology row).** Claims graded `[TRAD]` document *what the tradition does* and *why memory science says it works*; they name an identifiable source (for any ḥadīth: the collection and number, with grading where relevant), they are **scoped strictly to methodology**, and they **issue no fiqh ruling**. The app stays madhhab- and sect-neutral, is a *servant to the teacher* and the *sanad* chain, and flags "needs scholarly review" where appropriate (PRD §4, R2, R6; blueprint §1 science value 4). No row makes a cognitive, neurological, or academic-performance *promise* about the user (the hifz-specific neuro/academic literature is observational and confounded — see [`research/quran-memorization-research.md`](research/quran-memorization-research.md) — and is deliberately **not** surfaced as an in-app claim).

> **Evidence grades** (blueprint §3; best → weakest among empirical): **[MA]** meta-analysis / systematic review · **[RCT]** randomized experiment · **[EXP]** controlled cognitive experiment · **[CS]** classic foundational study · **[OBS]** observational / applied / field study · **[TEXT]** textbook / expert review / algorithm documentation · **[TRAD]** traditional / scholarly Islamic source. Preference among empirical: **MA > RCT/EXP > CS > OBS > TEXT**. A grade describes the **strength of the evidence behind the claim**, kept strictly separate from any **certainty** the app expresses about the user's own Quran.

## A. Memory & forgetting

The premise the whole product answers to: memorized material decays measurably and predictably, but a "forgotten" page is not gone — it is cheaply relearned if caught in time.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-001 | Memory fades on a predictable curve — steep at first, then slowly. | Decay is the engine's premise; the heat-map renders each page's slide down its own curve. Power-law retrievability (C-010) models the shape. | Ebbinghaus's forgetting curve, reproduced 130 years later in an independent subject and language ([Murre & Dros, 2015, *PLOS ONE*](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0120644)); origin ([Ebbinghaus, 1885/1913](https://psychclassics.yorku.ca/Ebbinghaus/index.htm)) | [EXP] / [CS] | Science screen; Progress (heat-map) | The classic curve is *n* = 1 on nonsense syllables; meaningful, over-learned text follows the *same shape, far slower* (C-008). |

Bull. ## B. Spacing & scheduling

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-006 | Revising with growing gaps retains far better than re-reading in one sitting. | The schedule distributes reviews; stability compounding widens intervals automatically. No retention *percentage* is ever promised. | Distributed practice reliably beats massed (mean *d* ≈ 0.46) ([Donovan & Radosevich, 1999, *J. Appl. ## C. Spaced-repetition engine (the math, stated honestly)

The few numbers the app simplifies are disclosed here as priors, not promises.

| ID | Claim (as the app states it) | Value / rule the app uses | Source(s) | Grade | App surface | Notes / caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-010 | The app models how recall fades with a standard, open spaced-repetition curve. | Power-law retrievability `R(t,S) = (1 + (19/81)·t/S)^(−0.5)`, so `R = 0.9` exactly when `t = S` (FSRS-style DSR backbone). | FSRS power-law curve, `DECAY = −0.5`, `FACTOR = 19/81` ([Open Spaced Repetition: *The Algorithm*](https://github.com/open-spaced-repetition/awesome-fsrs/wiki/The-Algorithm); [Expertium: *FSRS Algorithm*](https://expertium.github.io/Algorithm.html)) | [TEXT] | Science screen (glossed plainly); engine | Algorithm documentation, not an empirical claim; constants are a **starting prior**, tunable from the user's own lapse history (no ML, no network — C2). |
| C-011 | Each on-time review makes a page hold longer before it needs revising again. | On success, stability grows multiplicatively (`S' = S·SInc`), so safe intervals lengthen — the basis for mature juz moving to weekly/monthly. | Two-component (S, R) model: stability gain is largest when retrievability is low ([Woźniak: *Two components of memory*](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Two_components_of_memory); [*Algorithm SM-17*](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Algorithm_SM-17)); successful retrieval flattens the forgetting curve ([Wheeler et al., 2003, *Memory*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14982124/)) | [TEXT] / [EXP] | Science screen; engine | Glossed in plain words; the user sees a *cycle*, never `S`/`R`. |
| C-012 | Reviewing a page when it's a little weaker strengthens it more than reviewing it while still fresh. | The `e^{w·(1−R)}` desirable-difficulty term: lower-R reviews buy more stability — the engine schedules by predicted strength, not a fixed rota. | Storage- vs retrieval-strength: largest gains from successful retrieval when retrieval strength is low ([Bjork & Bjork, 2011, desirable difficulties](https://www.unh.edu/teaching-learning-resource-hub/sites/default/files/media/2023-06/itow-introducing-desirable-difficulties-into-practice-and-instruction-bjork-and-bjork.pdf)); spacing benefit holds even when reviews error ([Pashler et al., 2003, *JEP:LMC*](https://doi.org/10.1037/0278-7393.29.6.1051)) | [TEXT] / [EXP] | Science screen; engine | "Expanding intervals are inherently best" is *not* claimed — what matters is that the first review is delayed enough to be effortful ([Karpicke & Roediger, 2007, *JEP:LMC*](https://doi.org/10.1037/0278-7393.33.4.704)) [EXP]. |
| C-014 | The traditional revision cycle already *is* spaced repetition; the app only re-orders within it. | SR ordering is the silent page-selector inside a fixed traditional cycle; pure-cycle mode turns the reordering fully off. | Tradition's day-1/2/4/7/14-then-monthly ladder and 7-manzil khatm are hand-tuned expanding-interval schedules; Leitner/Pimsleur are special cases of a fittable curve ([Settles & Meeder, 2016, *ACL*](https://research.duolingo.com/papers/settles.acl16.pdf)) | [OBS] | Science screen; pure-cycle setting | MUST NOT present the algorithm as superseding the teacher or tradition (PRD R6, §7.11). |

## D. Retrieval & self-testing

Why the core daily act is reciting *from memory* before revealing the text — and why a teacher's correction counts for more than a self-rating.

| ID | Claim (as the app states it) | Value / rule the app uses | Source(s) | Grade | App surface | Notes / caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-018 | Reciting from memory protects your hifz far more than re-reading it. | Reveal-on-tap recite flow: page hidden → recite from memory → reveal → mark stumbles → grade. Read-along audio is a *correction aid after the attempt*, never equivalent revision. | The testing effect: free recall (*g* = 0.79) and cued recall (*g* = 0.70) far exceed recognition (*g* = 0.32); overall *g* = 0.50, 81% of comparisons favor testing ([Rowland, 2014, *Psychol. | C-019 | A strong page never becomes "done" — only continued recitation keeps it. | Every page is re-recited at least once per cycle no matter how strong; the engine never implies "safe to drop." | Continuing to *study* a learned item did nothing; continuing to *retrieve* it ~doubled one-week retention ([Karpicke & Roediger, 2008, *Science*, n = 40 pairs](https://www.science.org/doi/abs/10.1126/science.1152408)) | [EXP] | Science screen; engine invariant; heat-map | Direct scientific warrant for the "nothing is ever safe to drop" invariant (PRD §7.12); never re-read a strong page as a cheaper substitute. |

| C-021 | A teacher who hears your recitation and corrects it strengthens a page more than rating yourself. | Teacher sign-off `sourceConfidence = 1.0` moves state more than self-rating (`≈ 0.5`); teacher grade overrides self-rating and algorithmic state. | Feedback-moderator effect (teacher correction is retrieval + expert feedback) ([Rowland, 2014, *g* = 0.73 with feedback](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/264988491_The_Effect_of_Testing_Versus_Restudy_on_Retention_A_Meta-Analytic_Review_of_the_Testing_Effect)); talaqqī as the authoritative error signal ([Mohamad & Mohamad: Talaqqī & Mushāfaha](https://www.academia.edu/81052538/Concept_and_Execution_of_Talaqqi_and_Musyafahah_Method_in_Learning_Al_Quran)) | [MA] / [TRAD] | Science screen; teacher sign-off (PRD §8.2) | The app *aids* the teacher, never replaces; no microphone/ASR/AI mistake-detection (C2, R6). |
| C-022 | Re-reading feels like it works best, but reciting from memory is what actually holds. | The recite-from-memory flow is the path of least resistance; the science is stated once, calmly — no nag, no streak. | Learners reliably predict re-reading beats retrieval, the opposite of the result ([Karpicke & Blunt, 2011, *Science*](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1199327)); most students choose re-reading ([Karpicke, Butler & Roediger, 2009, *Memory*](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/09658210802647009)) | [EXP] / [OBS] | Science screen | Designed *against* the illusion of competence without coercion (PRD R3/C6) — never shame a user for re-reading. |

## E. Interference & mutashābihāt

Why similar-verse confusion is interference, not decay — and why the cure is back-to-back contrast, the opposite of ordinary spacing.

| ID | Claim (as the app states it) | Value / rule the app uses | Source(s) | Grade | App surface | Notes / caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-026 | Most forgetting comes from *interference* between similar passages, not the mere passing of time. | Mutashābihāt is a first-class subsystem alongside the decay engine; a confusion edge is a property of the *pair*, not one page. | Forgetting is cue-driven response competition, not disuse ([McGeoch, 1932, *Psychol. Rev.*](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1933-00060-001)); proactive + retroactive interference ([Underwood, 1957, *Psychol. Rev.*](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1958-01239-001)) | [CS] | Science screen; Mutashābihāt screen | No classic SR algorithm models interference — this is a genuine extension, not a parameter tweak. |
| C-027 | The more alike two passages are, the more they interfere — which is why repetition alone doesn't fix them. | The bundled dataset is scoped to **objective near-identical/identical wording only** (scholar-reviewed); drills target the distinguishing word. | Similarity gradient: synonyms interfere most, down to unrelated/nonsense ([McGeoch & McDonald, 1931, *Am. J. Psychol.*](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1415159)); transfer-and-retroaction surface ([Osgood, 1949, *Psychol. Rev.*](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1949-03900-001)) | [CS] | Science screen; Mutashābihāt screen | Dataset is **objective wording only**, never interpretive/thematic (PRD R4); needs scholarly sign-off before locking. |
| C-028 | To stop confusing two similar passages, practice them back-to-back so you can tell them apart. | When any group member is due, its sibling(s) are pulled into the **same session**, adjacent, for discrimination; spacing siblings apart is resisted. | Interleaving beats blocking *specifically* for confusable categories via discriminative contrast ([Kornell & Bjork, 2008, *Psychol. Sci.*](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02127.x); [Birnbaum et al., 2013, *Mem. & Cogn.*](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13421-012-0272-7); [Carvalho & Goldstone, 2014, *Mem. & Cogn.*](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13421-013-0371-0)) | [EXP] | Science screen; Mutashābihāt drills | Ordinary spacing is the *enemy* of discrimination here — the engine carves siblings out of normal spacing rules. |
| C-029 | Drilling only one of a confusable pair can weaken its twin — so we practice the whole group. | A "wrong-branch" stumble raises difficulty (and shortens intervals) on **all** group members; drills always present the contrasting pair, never one side alone. | Retrieval-induced forgetting: retrieving one competitor suppresses the unpracticed one, only under shared-cue competition ([Anderson, Bjork & Bjork, 1994, *JEP:LMC*](https://bjorklab.psych.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/13/2016/07/Anderson_RBjork_EBjork_1994.pdf)) | [EXP] | Mutashābihāt subsystem; engine | The error is a property of the *edge*, not the node; personalization is the user's own logged swaps only (no AI — C2). |
| C-030 | Confusing similar verses tends to get harder the more of the Quran you hold. | Mutashābihāt is made prominent for the near-complete ḥāfiẓ (P1), the most interference-exposed user. | Proactive interference rises with the number of prior lists learned ([Underwood, 1957](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1958-01239-001)); ḥifẓ accounts: errors increase "as more is memorized" ([GetItQan: *What is Mutashabihat*](https://getitqan.com/blog/what-is-mutashabihat)); >60% of surveyed students could not reliably distinguish them ([Mohd Yusoff et al., 2022, *IJARBSS*, n = 368](https://hrmars.com/papers_submitted/10821/tahfiz-education-in-malaysia-issues-and-problems-in-memorising-quranic-mutashabihat-verses-and-its-solution.pdf)) | [CS] / [TRAD] / [OBS] | Science screen; Mutashābihāt screen | Honest, not alarmist — explains *why* a finished ḥāfiẓ still stumbles; the survey is single-region self-report. |

## F. Serial recall & the page unit

Why the scheduled unit is the whole muṣḥaf page recited in flow, not an isolated āyah-card.

| ID | Claim (as the app states it) | Value / rule the app uses | Source(s) | Grade | App surface | Notes / caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C-031 | You memorize and recite the Quran in flowing pages, not as separate cards — so we revise it the same way. | One card = one muṣḥaf page (604 cards), carrying its own D/S/R; review is whole-page, in fixed order. | Recall is a chunked, hierarchically-planned, forward-cued production, not a bead-chain of independent items ([Lashley, 1951 / Rosenbaum et al., 2007](https://www.academia.edu/4453729/The_problem_of_serial_order_in_behavior_Lashley_s_legacy); [Henson et al., 1996, *QJEP*](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1080/713755612)); the chunk, not the item, is the unit ([Miller, 1956, *Psychol. Rev.*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Magical_Number_Seven,_Plus_or_Minus_Two)) | [CS] | Science screen; recite flow | Verse-level carding severs the serial cue and tests a retrieval path that doesn't exist in fluent recitation. |
| C-032 | Re-reciting the same page in its fixed order is exactly what cements it. | The scheduler returns the *same ordered page* at the right intervals; it never reorders verses or splices pages to "freshen" review. | The Hebb repetition effect: repeating a fixed sequence builds a long-term, order-specific trace ([Hebb, 1961 / Szmalec et al., 2009, *QJEP*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18785073/)) | [CS] | Engine; reader (fixed layout) | Reinforces immutable per-page layout (PRD §11): line breaks/āyah markers are the chunk scaffold, never recomputed. |
