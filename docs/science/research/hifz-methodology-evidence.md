# Traditional Hifz Methodology as Empirical Spaced Repetition

**Topic:** The classical three-track revision system (sabaq / sabqi / manzil), the seven manāzil weekly khatm, talaqqī and the sanad chain, and classical scholarly guidance on murājaʿa — read as a centuries-old, hand-tuned approximation of modern spaced repetition, and mapped onto the FSRS-style engine that powers Hifz Companion.

**Compiled:** 2026-06-16

This note is the evidence dossier behind the synthesis doc *10-traditional-hifz-methodology* and feeds the *Traditional methodology* group of `CLAIMS.md`. It is deliberately **sect-neutral** and **issues no fiqh ruling**: it documents *what the tradition does* and *why memory science says it works*, citing identifiable traditional and scholarly sources. Where a hadith is quoted, the collection and number are given. Where a regional practice is described, it is described as practice, not prescription. The convergence thesis — *the masters arrived empirically at expanding-interval retrieval practice* — validates the tradition; it never replaces the teacher (talaqqī) or claims authority over a scholar.

---

## What the evidence says

### 1. The three tracks are three phases of one revision lifecycle, not three algorithms

The dominant madrasa workflow — used by ḥuffāẓ worldwide for generations — structures every day around three named portions ([IslamicTuition: Sabaq, Sabqi and Manzil](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/); [Ilmify: Muraja'ah in Hifz](https://ilmify.app/blog/what-is-murajaah-quran-revision/)):

- **Sabaq** (السبق, "the lesson") — today's *new* memorization. For a beginner, typically **3–5 lines/day**, increasing with stamina ([IslamicTuition](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/)).
- **Sabqi** (سبقي, "my recent lesson") — the *recently* memorized material, roughly **the last week or two** (the rolling ~1–3 juzʾ), revised **daily or every other day** while it is still settling into long-term memory ([IslamicTuition](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/)).
- **Manzil / dhor** (منزل / دور, "the stage / the round") — the *long-term* cycling through everything already memorized beyond the recent window: earlier ajzāʾ, completed surahs, the whole-Quran bulk that "risks fading without regular attention" ([IslamicTuition](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/); [Ilmify: Sabak, Sabaq Para, and Dhor](https://ilmify.app/blog/sabak-sabaq-para-dhor-hifz-stages/)).

Critically, a page is not assigned permanently to a track: it **graduates** as it strengthens (sabaq → sabqi → manzil) and is **demoted back into active revision when it lapses** — "a forgotten manzil page rejoins revision." This is one lifecycle, distinguished by the maturity (stability) of the material, which is exactly how a difficulty/stability/retrievability (DSR) model already treats a single card's life ([Expertium: FSRS Algorithm](https://expertium.github.io/Algorithm.html)).

### 2. "Old before new" is the tradition's built-in spacing-and-retrieval discipline

The classical session order recites **old material before new** — sabqi and manzil are completed *before* the day's sabaq, and more time is allocated to old than to new ([IslamicTuition](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/); [Ilmify](https://ilmify.app/blog/what-is-murajaah-quran-revision/)). When a student finishes a few ajzāʾ, new memorization is often **paused entirely** to "protect everything already earned" ([IslamicTuition](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/)). Two well-documented memory mechanisms are encoded here:

- **Distributed practice (the spacing effect):** revising old material on a recurring schedule rather than re-massing the new lesson is precisely the manipulation Cepeda and colleagues' meta-analysis of 839 assessments across 317 experiments found reliably superior for long-term retention ([Cepeda et al., 2006](https://www.yorku.ca/ncepeda/publications/CPVWR2006.html)).
- **Retrieval practice:** murājaʿa is *recitation from memory*, i.e. effortful retrieval, which strengthens memory far more than passive re-reading — the testing effect. The tradition never "re-reads" the muṣḥaf as the primary act; it *produces* the text aloud.

The "more time on old than new" rule is the empirical ancestor of a **review-to-new ratio** (commonly stated as ~5:1) and of a hard daily-load cap on maintenance — the tradition's defense against new memorization outpacing sustainable revision.

### 3. The seven manāzil are a fixed weekly cycle — a literal "review the whole corpus every 7 days" rota

For those who wish to complete the Quran in a week, the text is divided into **seven portions called manāzil**, a division attributed to the early Kūfan reciter **Ḥamzah az-Zayyāt (d. 156 AH / 772 CE)** ([Wikipedia: Manzil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manzil)). The seven divisions are:

| Manzil | Surahs | Count |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | al-Fātiḥa (1) → an-Nisāʾ (4) | 4 |
| 2 | al-Māʾida (5) → at-Tawba (9) | 5 |
| 3 | Yūnus (10) → an-Naḥl (16) | 7 |
| 4 | al-Isrāʾ (17) → al-Furqān (25) | 9 |
| 5 | ash-Shuʿarāʾ (26) → Yā-Sīn (36) | 11 |
| 6 | aṣ-Ṣāffāt (37) → al-Ḥujurāt (49) | 13 |
| 7 | Qāf (50) → an-Nās (114) | 65 |

The portions are remembered by the mnemonic **"Fa-mī bi-shawqin"** (فمي بشوقٍ), whose seven consonants — **F, M, B, Sh, W, Q, N** — are the initials of the opening surah of each manzil (al-**F**ātiḥa, al-**M**āʾida, etc.) ([Wikipedia: Manzil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manzil); [Famibisyauqin](https://famibisyauqin.blogspot.com/2016/02/fami-bi-syauqin-mengkhatamkan-al-quran.html)). Read as scheduling, this is a **uniform 7-day cycle ceiling**: every part of the Quran is revisited at least once per week, no matter how strong it feels. Other common rotas are **30/15/10-day** khatm cycles (one juzʾ, two juzʾ, or three juzʾ per day), with a typical ḥāfiẓ keeping a floor of about **one juzʾ per day** of revision.

### 4. Expanding intervals — what the tradition tunes by hand — is exactly what the science recommends

The tradition tightens revision for fresh material and progressively *stretches* it for mature material: sabqi is revised daily/alternate-day; manzil is revisited weekly to monthly as it solidifies. This **expanding-interval** shape is not folklore — Cepeda et al.'s meta-analysis specifically compared expanding versus uniform schedules and found expanding intervals produced **better retention**, and that the optimal inter-study interval *grows* as the desired retention interval lengthens ([Cepeda et al., 2006](https://www.yorku.ca/ncepeda/publications/CPVWR2006.html)). FSRS encodes the same dynamic mathematically: each on-time successful review multiplies stability, so the safe interval lengthens automatically, and reviewing when retrievability is *lower* yields a *larger* stability gain — the desirable-difficulty effect of Bjork's storage-vs-retrieval-strength framework ([Expertium: FSRS Algorithm](https://expertium.github.io/Algorithm.html); [Bjork & Bjork: Desirable Difficulties](https://www.unh.edu/teaching-learning-resource-hub/sites/default/files/media/2023-06/itow-introducing-desirable-difficulties-into-practice-and-instruction-bjork-and-bjork.pdf)). The convergence is the thesis of this whole doc set: **the masters arrived at expanding-interval retrieval practice empirically, centuries before the experiments confirmed it.**

### 5. The Prophetic warning about forgetting is the tradition's "decay" axiom

The urgency of murājaʿa rests on explicit ḥadīth. Abū Mūsā al-Ashʿarī reported that the Prophet ﷺ said: *"Keep on reciting this Qur'an, for by the One in Whose Hand my soul is, the Qur'an slips away from memory faster than camels escaping their tying ropes"* (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 791, agreed upon) ([HadeethEnc 5907](https://hadeethenc.com/en/browse/hadith/5907)). And from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd, the Prophet ﷺ discouraged saying "I forgot such-and-such a verse" — rather "he has been *caused* to forget it" — and urged continuous review, "for it escapes from the hearts of men faster than camels [from their hobbles]" (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 5032, Book 66 Ḥadīth 54) ([Sunnah.com: Bukhari 5032](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:5032)). Memorized text *decays without active retrieval* — the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve, stated in seventh-century terms. This is the doctrinal root of the app's "nothing decays silently" contract.

### 6. The annual ʿarḍa — a Prophetic precedent for whole-corpus periodic review

Beyond daily revision, the tradition preserves a precedent for *full-corpus* periodic review (ʿarḍa, عَرْضَة). Abū Hurayra reported that **Gabriel (Jibrīl) used to review the Quran with the Prophet ﷺ once every year, and reviewed it twice in the year he passed away** (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 4998, Book 66 Ḥadīth 20) ([Sunnah.com: Bukhari 4998](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:4998)). This *al-ʿarḍa al-akhīra* (the final review) is the classical anchor for the practice of a complete annual/Ramadan run-through of everything one holds — i.e. a guaranteed maximum interval over the *entire* memorized corpus, the conceptual parent of a "cycle ceiling" that no page is allowed to exceed.

### 7. Talaqqī and the sanad: oral, teacher-verified transmission is the integrity layer

The Quran is transmitted **talaqqī** (تلقّي) and **mushāfaha** (مشافهة) — face-to-face, lip-to-lip, the student receiving directly from the teacher's recitation and the teacher correcting in real time ([Mohamad & Mohamad: Talaqqi and Musyafahah](https://www.academia.edu/81052538/Concept_and_Execution_of_Talaqqi_and_Musyafahah_Method_in_Learning_Al_Quran)). This method is held to descend from the Prophet ﷺ receiving from Gabriel, then transmitting to the Companions, in an unbroken chain. Mastery is certified by an **ijāza** carrying a **sanad/isnād** — a documented chain of teachers reaching back to the Prophet ﷺ ([Wikipedia: Ijazah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ijazah)). Two design consequences follow:

- **The error signal is human and authoritative.** Correctness is judged by a qualified human ear, not a machine; a teacher's correction is the ground-truth grade. An app that respects the tradition must let teacher sign-off *override* any self-rating or algorithmic state — and must add **no microphone, no ASR, no machine "mistake detection,"** because that would arrogate the teacher's role and break the talaqqī chain.
- **Self-rating is lower-confidence.** Because the authoritative grade is the teacher's, unverified self-assessment must move the schedule less aggressively than a teacher's verdict.

### 8. Mutashābihāt: interference, not decay, is the advanced ḥāfiẓ's main enemy

Classical and applied scholarship treats **mutashābihāt** (المتشابهات) — near-identical or identical passages differing by a word or two — as a first-class hazard that *worsens as more is memorized*, because the shared wording, rhythm and length cause the brain to merge traces and recite the wrong continuation ([Getitqan: What is Mutashabihat](https://getitqan.com/blog/what-is-mutashabihat); [Yusof et al.: Memorising Mutashabihat](https://www.academia.edu/106990183/Tahfiz_Education_in_Malaysia_Issues_and_Problems_in_Memorising_Quranic_Mutashabihat_Verses_and_its_Solution)). This is **interference**, the opposite of decay, and no classical SR algorithm models it. The cognitive remedy is **interleaved discrimination** — practising confusable items back-to-back so the brain rehearses telling them apart, which research shows helps most precisely when categories are confusable. The tradition's habit of deliberately juxtaposing the "similar verses" to fix the distinguishing word is the manual version of this; the app makes it a first-class subsystem.

### 9. The same skeleton appears across regional schools — terminology and intensity vary, the structure does not

Independent regional traditions converge on the same expanding-interval, retrieval-and-correction structure under different names:

- **Deobandi / South Asian:** the canonical sabaq / sabqi / manzil (dhor) triad, "old before new," with the corpus split into manāzil rotated on a fixed schedule ([IslamicTuition](https://islamictuition.us/blog/sabaq-sabqi-manzil-hifz-revision-system/)).
- **Mauritanian (maḥḍara):** writing the lesson on a wooden tablet (*lawḥ / luḥ*), reciting until memorized, then **repeating 300–500 times** counted on prayer-beads — overlearning to automaticity before moving on ([Tarteel: The Mauritanian Method](https://tarteel.ai/blog/quran-memorization-techniques-the-mauritanian-method/)).
- **Indonesian (takrār / tikrār):** systematic, ordered **repetition** (often ~40× per portion) before reciting to the teacher for verification ([Medium/HTMTQ: The Takrār Method](https://medium.com/how-to-memorise-the-quran/the-takrar-method-of-memorisation-in-madinah-al-munawwarah-schedules-62fe4a8d1179)).
- **Ottoman / Turkish-Bosnian ("stacking"):** memorize one page a day across all 30 ajzāʾ in rounds, "stacking" each new page on top, with explicit tracks for hard verses (*zor pişmiş*), easy verses (*kolay pişmiş*) and full repetition (*tekrar*) ([HTMTQ: The Ottoman Hifz Method](https://howtomemorisethequran.com/the-ottoman-hifz-method/)).

The lesson for a sect-neutral app: the **structure** (three maturities, expanding intervals, teacher verification, full-corpus cycle, weak-spot drilling) is universal; the **vocabulary** is regional. Labels must therefore be swappable string resources, not hard-coded.

### 10. Overlearning to automaticity is the tradition's path to near-100% retention

The Mauritanian 300–500 repetitions and the Indonesian tikrār counts are not superstition: they are **overlearning** — continued practice past the first errorless recall — which the memory literature links to slower forgetting and durable, automatic retrieval. The tradition does not chase a fragile probability target; it *overlearns* the sacred text and then *guarantees periodic review of everything* via the manzil cycle. This is the empirical basis for Hifz Companion's design choice to deliver near-100% retention through a **cycle ceiling plus a higher retention floor for prayer-critical / weak / mutashābihāt pages**, rather than a single brittle global retention number.

## Implications for Hifz Companion

1. **Keep tradition as the literal interface.** Surface the three named tracks (sabaq / sabqi / manzil) and named cycles (7-manzil weekly khatm, 1-juzʾ/day, etc.) as the user-facing day. The FSRS DSR engine is the *invisible page-selector* inside that fixed traditional shape — never a user-facing "retention slider" (Findings 1, 3; PRD §6.2, §7.1).

2. **Model the three tracks as one card's lifecycle, gated by stability.** A page graduates sabaq → sabqi → manzil as `S` grows and demotes back on a lapse — exactly the traditional "a forgotten manzil page rejoins revision." Do not build three separate algorithms (Finding 1; PRD §6.2, §7.4).

3. **Encode "old before new" and the review-to-new discipline.** Build the day Far → Near → New, make manzil/dhor due items mandatory (never silently dropped), and throttle new sabaq when projected maintenance load exceeds the budget — the live-math version of "protect what you've earned" (Finding 2; PRD §7.8, §7.9).

4. **Make the cycle ceiling the real guarantee, anchored in the seven manāzil and the ʿarḍa.** Every page's `due_at` is clamped to the chosen cycle (e.g. 7 or 30 days); the algorithm may only pull a page *forward*, never past its ceiling. This is the seven-manzil weekly rota and the annual ʿarḍa, expressed in code (Findings 3, 6; PRD §7.6, §7.12).

5. **Let expanding intervals emerge from the engine, not a fixed rota.** Stability compounding reproduces the tradition's "stretch mature material" behavior while concentrating effort on weak/confusable pages — same total budget, better allocated (Finding 4; PRD §7.3, §7.5).

6. **Treat forgetting as inevitable and never tell a ḥāfiẓ a page is "safe to drop."** The camel ḥadīth is the doctrinal form of the decay axiom; the heat-map makes invisible decay visible, and the engine is forbidden from ever implying a page no longer needs revision (Finding 5; PRD §7.12, R3).

7. **Make teacher sign-off (talaqqī) authoritative and add no machine listening.** Teacher grade `sourceConfidence = 1.0` and overrides self-rating and algorithmic state; self-rating is lower-confidence. No microphone, no ASR, no AI "mistake detection" — this is both a hard constraint (C2) and a point of *adab* toward the sanad chain (Finding 7; PRD §8, R6).

8. **Ship mutashābihāt as a first-class interleaving subsystem.** When a page in a confusion group is due, pull its sibling(s) into the *same* session for back-to-back discrimination; a wrong-branch slip raises difficulty on *all* group members. The dataset stays objective-wording-only and scholar-reviewed (Finding 8; PRD §9, R4).

9. **Make every track/cycle label a swappable string resource.** Because the structure is universal but the vocabulary is regional (Deobandi, Arab, Ottoman, Indonesian, Mauritanian), ship region-appropriate term-sets the user can switch, pending native-speaker + scholarly review (Finding 9; PRD §13.4).

10. **Deliver near-100% retention via overlearning + ceiling, not a magic number.** Raise the retention floor for prayer-critical / weak / previously-lapsed pages and lean on the cycle ceiling for the guarantee — the engineered form of the tradition's "overlearn, then cycle everything" (Finding 10; PRD §7.5, §7.6).

11. **Stay sect-neutral and issue no ruling.** Present methodology, name its traditional and scholarly sources, and flag anything needing a scholar's sign-off; the app is *servant to the teacher*, never a muftī (Findings 7, 9; PRD R2, §4 of blueprint values).
