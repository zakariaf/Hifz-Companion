# Design Principles

This document turns Mihrab's seven pillars (see [README.md](README.md)) into seven testable design principles, each grounded in real, web-verified evidence and each paired with the anti-patterns we refuse. It is the file every other doc in this system resolves to when a downstream decision is ambiguous: [material foundations](02-material-and-platform-foundations.md), [color](03-color-and-themes.md), [type](04-typography.md), [layout](05-layout-spacing-touch.md), [motion](06-motion-and-haptics.md), [components](07-components.md), [data-viz](08-data-visualization.md), [accessibility](09-accessibility-and-inclusivity.md), [privacy UX](10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md), [voice](11-voice-and-tone.md), [localization & RTL](12-localization-and-rtl.md), and [Islamic identity & adab](13-islamic-identity-and-adab.md). The product these principles serve is locked in [`../PRD.md`](../PRD.md): a fully offline, free (*ṣadaqah jāriyah*), no-AI, RTL Quran-memorization **retention** app for huffaz across Persian (fa), Kurdish Sorani (ckb), and Arabic (ar). Design tokens are referenced by **name** only; their concrete values live in the files that own them ([README → Token discipline](README.md)).

## The principles at a glance

| # | Principle | One-line statement |
|---|-----------|--------------------|
| 1 | Reverence first (adab) | The muṣḥaf is sacred; it is rendered faithfully and never decorated, gamified, or trivialized. |
| 2 | Calm, not cute | A quiet, adult instrument that lowers arousal — no streaks, badges, confetti, or manufactured urgency. |
| 3 | Tradition is the interface | The day looks like *sabaq / sabqi / manzil* a teacher recognizes; the algorithm is invisible. |
| 4 | Honest about decay | Nothing silently rots; the heat-map makes the invisible visible, and never says a page is "safe to drop." |
| 5 | Private by feel | No account, on-device, airplane-mode-forever — and the interface makes that guarantee perceptible. |
| 6 | RTL-native & multilingual | fa/ckb/ar are first-class RTL citizens with correct numerals, calendars, and Arabic-script type — never a bolted-on mode. |
| 7 | Servant to the teacher | *Talaqqī* and the *sanad* chain are respected; the app aids oral correction, never replaces it, and issues no ruling. |

These seven principles are the seven pillars of [README.md](README.md), restated as design rules with their anti-patterns attached. Where a principle's evidence overlaps another's (calm technology underwrites both *Calm, not cute* and *Servant to the teacher*; the muṣḥaf-page rendering underwrites both *Reverence* and *Tradition*), the cross-link is made explicit rather than the citation repeated as new.

## 1. Reverence first (adab)

**Statement.** An app holding the Quran is an act of *adab* before it is a product. The muṣḥaf is rendered page-faithfully through the bundled per-page glyph fonts and is never reflowed, decorated, animated, or gamified; chrome (counters, chips, markers) always defers to the words of Allah and never sits *on top of* them. Reverence is a set of design choices we can get right or wrong — it is intent-and-presentation, not device-gating.

**Evidence.**
- The most-trusted Quran apps converge on the same rendering decision: replicate the printed muṣḥaf page faithfully rather than reflow. The production stack is **glyph-based per-page fonts** — the KFGQPC/QPC fonts distributed through the Quranic Universal Library — where the **604 pages of the Madinah muṣḥaf each have their own font** and each glyph is a whole pre-drawn word, rendered without the OS shaper, so a digital page is the exact typeset image of the printed page ([QUL: Glyph-Based Fonts](https://qul.tarteel.ai/docs/glyph-based)). This is fidelity insurance, not aesthetics: asking the OS text shaper to lay out Uthmani text is the layer where broken ligatures and dropped or duplicated diacritics appear, and a single wrong diacritic in sacred text is unacceptable ([Quran Foundation: Font Rendering](https://api-docs.quran.foundation/docs/tutorials/fonts/font-rendering/); PRD R1, §11.2).
- The fixed page is also a *retention* feature, not nostalgia. Greentech Apps Foundation (a UK charity, 10M+ downloads) ships a dedicated "Mushaf Mode" because "consistency in mushaf layouts helps memorization by building photographic memory," and it is "quite popular among… people doing hifz" ([GTAF: Mushaf Mode](https://gtaf.org/blog/mushaf-mode-in-quran-app/)). For a ḥāfiẓ, *where on the page* a verse sits is part of the retrieval cue, so a reflowing reader destroys the cue a fixed page preserves.
- The most-admired Quran interfaces earn that status by *removing* UI. Ayah, winner of the Kuwait International Prize for the Holy Quran, is praised for "a clean, intuitive interface… without any visual clutter" that presents "the noble Quran itself with no dashboard," mimicking a physical muṣḥaf ([Ayah — App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/ayah-quran-app/id706037876)). In this category the highest compliment is that the interface disappears and only the muṣḥaf remains — the design-pattern equivalent of adab.
- Contemporary scholarship locates reverence in *intent and presentation*, not the ritual purity of the device: widely-held fatāwā find that reading Quran from a screen does not require wuḍūʾ, because "touching a screen is touching glass and circuits, not the sacred text itself," while approaching the text mindfully remains recommended as respect ([Mizan ul Muslimin](https://www.mizanulmuslimin.com/2025/03/do-you-need-wudu-for-quran.html); [Alhannah](https://blog.alhannah.com/can-you-read-the-quran-online-without-wudu-understanding-islamic-etiquette-in-the-digital-age/)). *(Summaries of scholarly positions; the app surfaces methodology, issues no fiqh ruling, and stays madhhab/sect-neutral.)*

**In practice.**

| Rule | Implementation |
|------|----------------|
| The font is the typeset page | Every page renders through the bundled KFGQPC per-page glyph fonts; the OS shaper is never asked to lay out Quran text. These glyph fonts are **not** `type.*` tokens — they are immutable bundled assets, referenced and never restyled ([README → Token discipline](README.md); [04-typography.md](04-typography.md)). |
| Overlays, never re-typesetting | Weak-line highlights, mutashābihāt anchors, and error positions are drawn as coordinates *over* the immutable glyph layer, never by editing or reconstructing text; zoom and the sepia/dark themes transform the rendered layer, not the words ([08-data-visualization.md](08-data-visualization.md); [03-color-and-themes.md](03-color-and-themes.md)). |
| The reader is "no dashboard" | When a page is shown, the muṣḥaf dominates and chrome recedes. Sepia and dark are first-class reading-comfort themes, not afterthoughts; a clean RTL page-turn is allowed, infinite continuous scroll is not (the page position is a retrieval cue). |
| No chrome on the sacred text | No badge, counter, mascot, `motion.*` celebration, or congratulatory effect ever sits on an āyah or marks "completing" a juz. Markers are diagnostic (weak/decaying), never decorative — this is the boundary [13-islamic-identity-and-adab.md](13-islamic-identity-and-adab.md) governs. |
| State the riwāyah | The muṣḥaf in use is shown as "Ḥafṣ ʿan ʿĀṣim — Madani muṣḥaf," never as "the Quran" in the absolute — itself an act of adab and neutrality (PRD R2). In all three locales (fa/ckb/ar) this label is a localized string, not baked-in chrome. |

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** ask the OS text shaper to lay out Quran text, or store/reconstruct the Uthmani text for rendering ([QUL](https://qul.tarteel.ai/docs/glyph-based); PRD R1); reflow the muṣḥaf, add ornamental borders, page-flip sound effects, or animation to the sacred text; place a counter, badge, mascot, or confetti on top of an āyah or on "finishing" a juz; gate access behind a wuḍūʾ check or present the device as itself the muṣḥaf; or bundle tafsīr, translation, or commentary that silently encodes a school of thought (PRD R2).

## 2. Calm, not cute

**Statement.** Mihrab is a quiet, adult instrument whose job is to lower arousal, not farm engagement. A daily ritual carrying spiritual weight should feel like peace of mind: a low-arousal palette, generous space, restrained motion, no streaks, no badges, no points, no confetti, no manufactured urgency, and no exclamation marks in product copy. The app's success metric is the user's trust and the Quran's retention — never daily-active-use or session length.

**Evidence.**
- This is psychophysics, not taste: emotional arousal is driven primarily by **saturation** (upward) and brightness, not hue, in the published color-emotion regression ([Valdez & Mehrabian, 1994](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1995-08699-001)). A calm worship surface is therefore a desaturated one, and saturated full-screen color is an arousal spike to avoid (palette owned by [03-color-and-themes.md](03-color-and-themes.md)).
- Bolting extrinsic rewards onto an already-intrinsically-meaningful act *reduces* intrinsic motivation — the **overjustification effect**. The definitive evidence is the meta-analysis of **128 experiments** by Deci, Koestner & Ryan: engagement-, completion-, and performance-contingent tangible rewards all significantly undermined free-choice intrinsic motivation, while non-controlling **positive feedback** was the one thing that *enhanced* it ([Deci, Koestner & Ryan, 1999](https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/0033-2909.125.6.627)). A badge for finishing a juz risks replacing the worshipper's *lillāh* reason with the app's token; honest "this page is solid, that one is decaying" feedback does not.
- Streaks operate by **loss aversion**, turning a missed day into a felt punishment even though nothing tangible is taken; the residue is guilt and anxiety manufactured by the design ([UX Magazine, 2026](https://uxmag.com/articles/the-psychology-of-hot-streak-game-design-how-to-keep-players-coming-back-every-day-without-shame)). For a ḥāfiẓ — for whom losing hifz already carries real spiritual weight — a broken-streak shame state *weaponizes an existing fear*, exactly the move PRD R3 forbids.
- The mere act of measuring an activity reduces enjoyment of it, lowering subsequent intrinsic motivation and well-being even with no reward attached — quantification makes an enjoyable activity "feel more like work" ([Etkin, 2016](https://doi.org/10.1093/jcr/ucv095)). The escape hatch is that measurement is benign when it is *integral, diagnostic* information rather than the worship being scored: a decay diagnosis is fine, a "pages revised this week" vanity counter is not.
- Leaderboards are the game element most often linked to harm and demotivate exactly the strugglers ([Toda, Valle & Isotani, 2018](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-97934-2_9)); and even *framing* an activity as a game — with no mechanics at all — changes the psychological relationship to it ([Lieberoth, 2015](https://doi.org/10.1177/1555412014559978)), so cute "playfulness" is not harmless decoration over a sacred act.
- The positive tradition is **calm technology**: technology "that informs but doesn't demand our focus or attention," living in the periphery and surfacing only when needed, on the principle that "technology should require the smallest possible amount of attention" ([Weiser & Brown, 1996](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology); [Case, 2015](https://calmtech.com/)). This restraint is *iḥsān* expressed in interaction terms, and it is the citable design stance behind "calm, not cute."

**In practice.**
- **No streaks, ever** — not even "gentle" ones. We never count consecutive days, show a flame/counter, or say "don't break your chain." The optional continuity indicator PRD §12.5 allows is private, opt-in, framed as information rather than a possession to defend, and silenceable to zero presence. There is no streak component in [07-components.md](07-components.md).
- **No badges, points, XP, levels, or trophies** on recitation or revision. There is no `reward.*` token and no celebration animation tier; reaching a milestone is acknowledged with calm factual copy ("This juz is now in long-cycle revision"), never a prize.
- **No leaderboards or social comparison of worship** — full stop. Local multi-profile (teacher/family) is bookkeeping, never a ranking (PRD R3).
- **Diagnose, don't score.** The retention heat-map is honest decay diagnosis ("where is your Quran weakening?") using a calm sequential ramp and non-color encoding (`color.heatmap.strong … color.heatmap.faded`, owned by [03-color-and-themes.md](03-color-and-themes.md)), never a "score to improve" or a green-completion game ([08-data-visualization.md](08-data-visualization.md)).
- **Motion is informative, not celebratory.** Low-arousal durations and curves (`motion.duration.*`, `motion.curve.*`), full Reduce-Motion support, no confetti or fanfare anywhere including a completed khatm — caps owned by [06-motion-and-haptics.md](06-motion-and-haptics.md).
- **Copy states facts and stops** — no exclamation marks in system-initiated text; the single optional daily reminder is neutral and easily silenced (rules owned by [11-voice-and-tone.md](11-voice-and-tone.md)).

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** ship a streak counter, a "don't lose your streak" notification, or a broken-streak shame state ([UX Magazine, 2026](https://uxmag.com/articles/the-psychology-of-hot-streak-game-design-how-to-keep-players-coming-back-every-day-without-shame); PRD R3); award points, XP, badges, levels, or trophies for reciting, revising, or completing any portion of the Quran ([Deci, Koestner & Ryan, 1999](https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/0033-2909.125.6.627)); show a leaderboard or any public/social ranking of memorization; fire confetti or celebratory animation on a juz, khatm, or session; frame revision as a game (mascots, "level up your hifz") ([Lieberoth, 2015](https://doi.org/10.1177/1555412014559978)); or optimize any surface for daily-active-use, session length, or "engagement" as a success metric ([Case, 2015](https://calmtech.com/)).

## 3. Tradition is the interface

**Statement.** The visible surface is the traditional revision workflow a teacher already recognizes — *sabaq / sabqi / manzil*, recited old-before-new, in named cycles ("7-Manzil weekly khatm," "1 juz/day"). The FSRS-style scheduler is demoted to a silent page-selector inside a fixed-shape traditional day: it may only **order** weak pages first and pull a decaying page *forward*, never reshape the day or tell the user "what to do" in algorithm language. Tradition wins on the surface; the math wins only in the invisible ordering.

**Evidence.**
- The traditional muraja'ah system is itself an empirical, hand-tuned spaced-repetition schedule: the three tiers map onto increasing-interval review (sabaq = fresh learning, sabqi = recent rising-stability material, manzil/dhor = mature high-stability cards), and the familiar "5:1" review-to-new ratio and 7-manzil weekly cycle are fixed approximations of optimal review pressure ([RESEARCH-FINDINGS.md §3](../../research/RESEARCH-FINDINGS.md); [ilmify: What is Murajaah](https://ilmify.app/blog/what-is-murajaah-quran-revision/)). FSRS does not replace this model; it makes the familiar mental model adaptive underneath. Keeping the recognizable surface is therefore an *accuracy* decision as much as a trust one.
- The category's most-admired UIs prove that "book-like, no dashboard" is what serious users want: Ayah is praised precisely for presenting "the noble Quran itself with no dashboard" rather than an instrument panel ([Ayah — App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/ayah-quran-app/id706037876)), and distraction-free "Mushaf Mode" is what "students of knowledge and people doing hifz" actually use ([GTAF: Mushaf Mode](https://gtaf.org/blog/mushaf-mode-in-quran-app/)). A retention-slider-and-graph dashboard would be the opposite of this evidence.
- Autonomy is a basic psychological need for durable motivation ([Ryan & Deci, 2000](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/SDT/documents/2000_RyanDeci_SDT.pdf)). Presenting the day as a tradition the user already accepts — and letting them pick a *named cycle* and a fully manual "pure-cycle" mode — supports autonomy; presenting it as an "optimal" the algorithm dictates erodes it. The Islamic-app HCI study finds the field generally neglects autonomy, competence, and relatedness, over-investing in extrinsic mechanics instead ([Kabir, Kabir & Islam, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.02061)).

**In practice.**

| Rule | Implementation |
|------|----------------|
| Three familiar buckets, one engine | The Today screen groups revision into New / Near-revision / Far-revision (localized *sabaq / sabqi / manzil*), recited Far → Near → New (old before new). These are three *lifecycle phases of one page card*, not three algorithms (PRD §6.2, §7.8) — surfaced via the track chips in [07-components.md](07-components.md). |
| Named cycles, never a retention slider | Users choose "7-Manzil weekly khatm," "1 juz/day," "½ juz/day," or a custom/pure-cycle mode — never a `target_R` dial (PRD §7.11, §15.1). The math's target retention is internal and never user-facing. |
| The algorithm only orders and pulls forward | The scheduler may surface the weakest/most-due pages first and pull a decaying page forward within the accepted track; it can never push a page past its cycle ceiling or reshape the traditional day (PRD §7.6, §7.12). |
| Localizable terminology | The *sabaq/sabqi/manzil* vocabulary is regional and lives in swappable ARB term-sets per locale (ar/fa/ckb), with a regional-override layer — never hardcoded; the ckb defaults await native + scholarly review (PRD §13.4, §21). Owned by [12-localization-and-rtl.md](12-localization-and-rtl.md). |
| A teacher can read the day at a glance | Track chips, grade verbs ("recited clean," "needed help"), and cycle names use the words a teacher uses, so the daily session is legible to someone who never opens the settings (PRD §6.3; [11-voice-and-tone.md](11-voice-and-tone.md)). |

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** expose a "retention slider," an FSRS difficulty/stability number, or algorithm jargon as the primary control surface; replace the *sabaq/sabqi/manzil* mental model with a generic "due cards" queue; let the scheduler reshape the traditional day's shape rather than merely order within it (PRD §7.12); build a dashboard-first home that buries the muṣḥaf behind charts; or hardcode the regional revision terminology so a region cannot switch to its own vocabulary (PRD §13.4).

## 4. Honest about decay

**Statement.** Nothing silently rots. The whole-Quran retention heat-map makes the invisible state of 604 pages visible at a glance, and the app is forbidden from ever telling a ḥāfiẓ a page is "safe to drop." Decay is shown *honestly* — the calm green simply receding toward a muted neutral — but never *alarmingly*: no red scoreboard, no shame-pile, no false precision. A juz's health leans to its weakest page, because one weak page is what fails you in ṣalāh.

**Evidence.**
- The heat-map is the correct encoding for "show me my whole Quran in one glance": a dense grid of color-coded cells lets the eye take in a large matrix and find hot spots without reading any number ([Wilkinson & Friendly, 2009](https://www.datavis.ca/papers/HeatmapHistory-tas.2009.pdf)), and Shneiderman's mantra — overview, zoom and filter, details-on-demand — maps directly onto whole-Quran → juz → page ([Shneiderman, 1996](https://www.cs.umd.edu/~ben/papers/Shneiderman1996eyes.pdf)).
- Strength is *ordered* data, so it needs a **sequential single-hue lightness ramp**, which is read as monotonic order, survives greyscale, and survives color-vision deficiency because order is carried by luminance not hue ([Brewer, Hatchard & Harrower, 2003](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1559/152304003100010929)). The rainbow/jet colormap is the wrong default — it "confuses viewers through its lack of perceptual ordering, obscures data through its uncontrolled luminance variation, and actively misleads… through… non-data-dependent gradients" ([Borland & Taylor, 2007](https://doi.org/10.1109/MCG.2007.323435)).
- Avoiding alarm is measured, not soft: red carries learned threat meaning and primes **avoidance motivation** in evaluation contexts ([Elliot & Maier, 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4383146/)), and arousal rises with saturation ([Valdez & Mehrabian, 1994](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1995-08699-001)), so a grid of saturated-red "rotting" pages would do exactly the two things the PRD prohibits — spike anxiety and frame revision as fear-driven avoidance (PRD R3).
- The numbers the app shows are *predictions*, not measurements (FSRS retrievability from low-confidence priors and self-ratings, PRD §7.3, §8.1), so presenting a crisp "87%" is false precision. A **Value-Suppressing Uncertainty Palette** desaturates cells where confidence is low, which makes people weight uncertainty more heavily and decide more cautiously ([Correll, Moritz & Heer, 2018](https://www.domoritz.de/papers/2018-VSUPs-CHI.pdf)).
- State must never be conveyed by color alone: WCAG 2.2 SC 1.4.1 forbids it and SC 1.4.11 sets a 3:1 non-text contrast floor for tiles ([W3C: WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/)), so every cell is encoded redundantly — color *and* a localized number *and* a label — which is simultaneously the colorblind-safe design and the honest one.

**In practice.**
- **The whole-Quran heat-map is the surface of the Progress screen**, all 604 pages grouped by 30 juz in muṣḥaf order, RTL, with exact strength, due date, and history living behind a tap — overview → zoom → details, not the reverse (PRD §12.5; [08-data-visualization.md](08-data-visualization.md)).
- **Strength is a single-hue lightness ramp of the base green** (`color.heatmap.strong … color.heatmap.faded`, owned by [03-color-and-themes.md](03-color-and-themes.md)): strong pages a deeper calm green, weakening pages fading toward a light desaturated neutral. Never a red→green gradient, never rainbow/jet.
- **Uncertain strength renders muted/greyed**, growing vivid only as confident reviews accumulate (especially teacher sign-offs, `sourceConfidence = 1.0`) — the VSUP principle made literal, and the visible analogue of the conservative-priors rule (PRD §7.10).
- **A juz tile leans to its weakest page** via the min-leaning roll-up, never the average — averaging would let one rotting page hide inside a strong juz, the exact silent decay the product exists to prevent (PRD §10.3, §7.12). Documented so no future contributor "improves" it into a mean.
- **Every state carries color + number + label**; where a page is named "weak" it comes with a concrete retrievability range and a due date in the user's calendar and numerals (PRD §18) — never color alone, never a bare percentage.
- **Decay framing is quiet maintenance**, not loss: "these pages are ready for revision," never "you are losing your Quran" (copy owned by [11-voice-and-tone.md](11-voice-and-tone.md); PRD §14).

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** tell a ḥāfiẓ a page is "safe to stop revising," or silently drop a due manzil item (PRD §7.12); render decay as a saturated-red scoreboard or a "you failed" overdue pile; use the rainbow/jet colormap or any hand-picked multi-hue gradient ([Borland & Taylor, 2007](https://doi.org/10.1109/MCG.2007.323435)); average a juz's health so a single weak page disappears (PRD §10.3); show a model prediction as a false-precise measured fact; or let the heat-map become a streak, a score, or a "100% green!" celebration ([08-data-visualization.md](08-data-visualization.md); PRD R3).

## 5. Private by feel

**Statement.** Privacy here is structural *and* meant to be felt. There is no account, no login, no PII, no telemetry, no microphone, and no backend we operate; the only network use is a one-time, checksum-verified HTTPS download of *public* Quran assets, after which the app runs in airplane mode forever (PRD §17, C1, R5). Because an invisible guarantee defaults to distrust, the interface must make the guarantee *perceptible* and *checkable* — no sync spinners, no account nags, plain-language claims stated as verifiable facts.

**Evidence.**
- This audience has been burned, and trust here is religious, not cosmetic: **Muslim Pro** (~95M downloads) was found in 2020 sending users' precise location to a data broker whose data reached the US military, undisclosed in the privacy policy and with no first-open dialog ([Cox, 2020, Vice/Motherboard](https://www.vice.com/en/article/muslim-pro-location-data-military-xmode/)). The peer-reviewed category study calls explicitly for "ethical and transparent data practices in religious technologies to maintain user trust" ([Kabir, Kabir & Islam, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.02061)).
- The hard problem is that an invisible guarantee is an untrusted one: users reason through **folk models** of how systems work, and if the mental model of "an app on my phone" assumes a server logging them, the *absence* of that server is invisible and default distrust wins by inertia ([Wash, 2010](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1837110.1837125)). Perceptibility, not mere correctness, is the design deliverable.
- Salient, concrete privacy information at the decision moment changes behavior — people choose and even pay for verified privacy ([Tsai, Egelman, Cranor & Acquisti, 2011](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/isre.1090.0260)) — and a truthful **"Data Not Collected"** posture measurably increases willingness to install ([Balash et al., 2024](https://www.usenix.org/conference/soups2024/presentation/balash)). But label terminology is widely misunderstood, so the guarantee must be restated in plain language *inside* the app ([Zhang et al., 2022](https://petsymposium.org/popets/2022/popets-2022-0106.php)).
- Vague reassurance backfires with a privacy-literate audience: "we care about your privacy" functions as reputation management and is parsed critically; privacy communication must be **specific, verifiable, and accountable** ([Song et al., 2024](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3613904.3642384)). Store self-disclosures are themselves untrustworthy — roughly 80% of top apps' Play "Data Safety" labels were found false or misleading ([Mozilla, 2023](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/blog/mozilla-study-data-privacy-labels-for-most-top-apps-in-google-play-store-are-false-or-misleading/)) — so verifiability must be structural (open source, public checksum-pinned asset URLs), not self-reported.

**In practice.**
- **Lead with the structural fact as a checkable claim, never a feeling.** Onboarding and the Privacy screen say what is true by construction in plain language — no account, nothing leaves this phone, no microphone, works in airplane mode — preferring "the app has no network code after setup — try airplane mode" over "we value your privacy" ([Song et al., 2024](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3613904.3642384)). Detailed treatment in [10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md](10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md).
- **No outward-pointing UI.** No sync spinners, no cloud iconography, no "invite," no account surface anywhere; the only export is a user-held local backup file the app moves nowhere (PRD §16).
- **The few network actions are framed honestly, in context.** The core-pack and reciter-pack downloads each get an in-context line — "Downloading the Quran files once from a public open-source repository. This sends nothing about you." — never a vague "connecting…" (PRD §11.1.1).
- **The missing microphone is a stated protection, not a silent gap.** "Recitation is judged by you or your teacher; the app cannot listen" — which also protects women's privacy by construction (PRD §8.3, R5).
- **Erasure is real and immediate.** One action wipes all local data; because there is no server, "delete" actually deletes, with nothing recoverable elsewhere (PRD §16) — a guarantee most apps cannot truthfully make.
- **The safe path is the default path** for the few real choices (notifications, optional reciter audio, backup encryption): protective default, the safe choice visually primary, the consequential action behind one deliberate step, explained in a single plain sentence.

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** ship an analytics, ads, crash-reporting, or backend SDK that phones home (a build-time check enforces this, PRD §19.3); require an account, login, or any PII to use the app; add a sync spinner, cloud-backup default, or account nag that implies data leaves the device; publish rhetorical "your privacy is paramount" statements in place of checkable facts ([Song et al., 2024](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3613904.3642384)); rely on the store's self-reported "Data Safety" form as proof of privacy ([Mozilla, 2023](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/blog/mozilla-study-data-privacy-labels-for-most-top-apps-in-google-play-store-are-false-or-misleading/)); or ever add a microphone, recording, or speech-to-text path (PRD C2, R5).

## 6. RTL-native & multilingual

**Statement.** Persian (fa), Kurdish Sorani (ckb), and Arabic (ar) are first-class right-to-left citizens, not a bolted-on mode. Layout is RTL by construction with logical (start/end) insets everywhere; numerals, calendars, and Arabic-script type are locale-correct; mixed Latin/numeric runs are bidi-isolated; and the UI typeface is deliberately, visibly distinct from the immutable QPC muṣḥaf type. Sorani is fully supported, with its extra letters glyph-verified before any font is locked.

**Evidence.**
- Legibility is governed by familiarity — "you read best what you read most" — so the UI must use the letterform skeleton these readers meet everywhere, and the muṣḥaf must keep its long-familiar traditional forms ([Nedeljković, Jovančić & Pušnik, 2020, *J. Eye Movement Research*](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7963459/)). This is the strongest evidence-based argument both for RTL-native UI type and for the strict UI/sacred type split.
- The three languages share one script but **Sorani extends the Arabic letter set** and is "almost a true alphabet… in which vowels are given the same treatment as consonants" ([Wikipedia: Kurdish alphabets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kurdish_alphabets)). A font that renders Persian and Arabic flawlessly can still drop `ە` (U+06D5), `ڕ`, `ڵ`, or `ۆ` to tofu, and uses the Kurdish kaf `ک` (U+06A9), not standard-Arabic kaf U+0643 — so coverage must be verified glyph-by-glyph, not assumed from "supports Arabic" ([r12a/W3C: Sorani notes](https://r12a.github.io/scripts/arab/ckb.html)).
- Arabic-script text has script-specific layout requirements: ascenders and descenders extend much further than Latin, so vertical spacing needs care ([W3C: Arabic & Persian Layout Requirements](https://www.w3.org/International/alreq/)); inter-letter spacing on *joined* script "creates undesirable results" and must not be applied ([W3C: alreq](https://www.w3.org/International/alreq/)); and a generous line-height (≈1.5) is both the accessibility floor and the comfortable setting for stacked marks and Sorani vowel letters.
- A credible OFL default exists but must be pinned to a verified version: **Vazirmatn** (variable, SIL OFL 1.1) gained Kurdish coverage over time but older packaging still lists only "Persian/Arabic," so the bundled version must be tested to render every Sorani codepoint before locking ([GitHub: rastikerdar/vazirmatn](https://github.com/rastikerdar/vazirmatn)) — exactly the gate PRD §13.5 requires.

**In practice.**

| Rule | Implementation |
|------|----------------|
| RTL by construction | `Directionality.rtl` app-wide; logical (start/end) insets only, never hardcoded left/right; directional icons, progress, and the bottom nav are mirrored (rightmost = home) (PRD §13.2; [12-localization-and-rtl.md](12-localization-and-rtl.md)). |
| Bidi isolation for mixed runs | Page numbers, "Juz 7," and Latin technical strings inside RTL text use Unicode FSI/PDI via Flutter `Bidi`/`Directionality`, so a number never flips its surrounding sentence (PRD §13.2). |
| Locale numerals and calendars | Persian/Kurdish render Extended Arabic-Indic (۰۱۲۳…), Arabic renders Arabic-Indic (٠١٢٣…) via `intl` `NumberFormat`; due/last-reviewed dates render in Hijri / Solar-Hijri-Jalālī / Gregorian per the user's choice — never raw ASCII digits concatenated into a localized string (PRD §13.3). |
| Verified Sorani type | A bundled Perso-Arabic UI font whose Sorani coverage (پ چ ژ ڤ ک گ ڕ ڵ ۆ ێ ە ھ) is CI-verified glyph-by-glyph before locking; `type.*` sizing sits at the larger end (≥ ~14sp) with ~1.5 line-height for stacked marks (PRD §13.5; owned by [04-typography.md](04-typography.md)). |
| UI type is never the muṣḥaf type | The localized `type.*` UI font is visibly different from the immutable QPC glyph fonts; the OS-shaper ban applies *only* to Quran glyphs, while UI text shapes normally — the most important distinction in the system (Pillar 1; [04-typography.md](04-typography.md)). |
| Full localization, no orphan strings | 100% ARB coverage gated in CI (no missing keys, no hardcoded user-facing strings), with RTL golden screenshots per locale (PRD §13.6, §20.5). |

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** treat RTL as a mirror-flip afterthought of an LTR design, or hardcode left/right insets; ship a UI font without glyph-by-glyph verification of every Sorani codepoint (PRD §13.5); render ASCII digits where the locale expects Extended Arabic-Indic or Arabic-Indic numerals; let an embedded Latin word or number flip its surrounding RTL sentence (missing FSI/PDI isolation); set Arabic-script body type at Latin "small" sizes or apply `letterSpacing` to joined script; or blur the line between UI type and the sacred QPC glyph fonts (Pillar 1, PRD R1).

## 7. Servant to the teacher

**Statement.** The app is a servant to *talaqqī* and the *sanad* chain — it aids oral correction and never replaces or claims authority over it. A teacher's sign-off is a first-class grade that overrides any self-rating and any algorithmic state; the app issues no fiqh ruling, stays madhhab/sect-neutral, and its copy consistently frames the tool as an aid to revision, never an authority over the *qārī* or the teacher.

**Evidence.**
- The strongest design lever for this voice is the science of **reactance**: controlling, commanding language provokes psychological reactance and can boomerang, whereas autonomy-supportive, empathetic framing persuades without backfiring ([Miller et al., 2007, *Human Communication Research*](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1468-2958.2007.00297.x)). An app that "tells" a ḥāfiẓ what is correct in sacred recitation would be both presumptuous and counterproductive; deferring to the human teacher is the persuasive *and* the respectful choice.
- Gamified accountability is a hollow substitute for the human relationship: reviewers of memorization apps note that a human teacher "provides accountability that streaks and badges can only imitate" ([Tarteel — App Store reviews](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/tarteel-ai-quran-memorization/id1391009396)). The honest response is not to fake the relationship with mechanics but to defer to it structurally.
- **Relatedness** — connection to others — is one of the three basic needs for durable motivation ([Ryan & Deci, 2000](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/SDT/documents/2000_RyanDeci_SDT.pdf)), and the Islamic-app HCI study finds the field neglects it ([Kabir, Kabir & Islam, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.02061)). First-class teacher sign-off is how Mihrab feeds relatedness honestly: it honors the *sanad* chain the app can only serve, never replace.
- The product is structurally no-AI by constraint — grading is self-rating and on-device human sign-off only, with no audio recognition (PRD C2, §8.3) — so the design must make the human, not a model, the authority on correctness.

**In practice.**

| Rule | Implementation |
|------|----------------|
| Teacher sign-off overrides the machine | A teacher's grade (`source = teacher`, `sourceConfidence = 1.0`) sets weak-flags, can graduate or demote a page, and supersedes self-rating and algorithmic state for that page (PRD §8.2, §7.12). Self-rating carries lower confidence and moves state less. |
| Local halaqa, never a server dashboard | "Teacher" and "parent" are *local multi-profile* on one device (or via exported reports), with an append-only `review_log` and optional teacher label that respects the *sanad* idea without any backend (PRD §8.2, §15.3). |
| Aid, never authority — in copy | Copy consistently frames the app as an aid to revision; it never claims to verify recitation, never issues a fiqh ruling, and stays madhhab/sect-neutral, with methodology surfaced and "needs scholarly review" flagged where apt ([11-voice-and-tone.md](11-voice-and-tone.md); [13-islamic-identity-and-adab.md](13-islamic-identity-and-adab.md)). |
| Autonomy-supportive, never controlling | Language states what is due and why (informational, competence-supporting), never pressures ("you must," "you're falling behind") — reactance-aware framing owned by [11-voice-and-tone.md](11-voice-and-tone.md). |
| No microphone, by construction | Recitation correctness is judged by a human (the ḥāfiẓ or teacher), exactly as the tradition does; there is no ASR, no recording, no "listen and detect mistakes" (PRD C2, R5). |

**Anti-patterns — we will never:** claim the app verifies recitation or "detects mistakes," or let an algorithm override a teacher's sign-off (PRD §7.12); issue a fiqh ruling, take a madhhab/sect position, or present methodology as a verdict; use controlling or commanding language about a user's recitation ([Miller et al., 2007](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1468-2958.2007.00297.x)); simulate the teacher relationship with streaks, badges, or a "score" standing in for accountability ([Tarteel reviews](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/tarteel-ai-quran-memorization/id1391009396)); build a server-side teacher dashboard or any cloud sharing of student data (PRD §15.3, C1); or add a microphone or speech-recognition path to grade recitation (PRD C2, R5).
