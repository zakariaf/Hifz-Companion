# E13-T08 — No-dashboard reader chrome: edge-receding auto-hiding controls, no gamification/ornament/piety gate on the page

## Goal

The reader becomes "no dashboard": the words of Allah dominate the surface and every control recedes to the edges, fades out on a calm timer (and on a tap on the page), and returns calmly on a tap — never with celebration, never gating the page. `packages/features/lib/src/mushaf/widgets/mushaf_chrome.dart` ships a `MushafChrome` that arranges the existing reader controls (the riwāyah/edition label and About entry from E13-T07; the zoom and light/sepia/dark theme toggles from E13-T06; the jump-to/page affordances over the E13-T03 pager) into a thin, dismissible edge layer over the immutable page, driven by a small visibility state (`chromeVisible`) with a fade transition at `motion.duration.short`. This task is the **conscience/restraint pass on the reader surface**: it asserts — in code, in tests, and in a checklist — that no badge, counter, confetti, glow, ornamental border over an āyah, page-flip sound/haptic fanfare, streak, "pages read today", celebration, or wuḍūʾ/piety gate ever sits on or guards the page; that no raw D/S/R or percentage is surfaced; and that nothing is ever marked droppable, optional, "done", or "mastered". It draws no new control and computes nothing — it positions and calmly hides what E13-T03/T06/T07 already built, and is the place the adab/text-integrity "no decoration on the sacred surface" rule is enforced for the whole reader.

## Implementation notes

TEST-FIRST: the "no decoration/gamification/piety-gate on the sacred surface" guarantees are correctness-critical adab (R1/R3 are release-blocking — a celebratory glyph over an āyah or a wuḍūʾ gate ends the project). Write the chrome-presence guard test and the auto-hide widget tests in the Tests section **before** the `MushafChrome` body.

1. **File / anatomy** (eng-add-feature-module, established by E13-T01): a leaf widget under the reader feature — `packages/features/lib/src/mushaf/widgets/mushaf_chrome.dart`, one primary public type `MushafChrome` (`ConsumerWidget`; `ConsumerStatefulWidget` only if it owns the auto-hide `Timer`/`AnimationController` as a disposable resource — it holds no business state). It composes E13-T03's pager, E13-T06's controls, and E13-T07's riwāyah/About chrome; it imports `flutter`/`riverpod` and the reader-state store only — **no** `drift`/DAO, **no** `package:http`/`dio`/`HttpClient`, **no** engine-mutation symbol, **no** audio/mic package, and it never touches E05's glyph layer or overlay painter directly.

2. **Edge layout, page-dominant** (ds-13 §3; ds-04 §1): a `Stack` with E13-T03's `MushafPager` as the **base/fill** layer and the controls as thin `Positioned`/`SafeArea` bands at the logical `start`/`end`/top/bottom **edges** only — never a panel over the page interior, never an overlay tinting or bordering the glyph area. Use logical insets (`EdgeInsetsDirectional`, `PositionedDirectional`) so the bands sit correctly in fa/ckb/ar RTL; reference `space.*`/`color.*`/`type.*`/`touch.min` token **names** only (values owned by E10/the design-system). The riwāyah label (E13-T07) renders in a top or bottom edge band; the zoom/theme controls (E13-T06) and the jump-to/page affordances render in an opposite edge band. Nothing the chrome draws covers, frames, glows behind, or decorates the words.

3. **Calm auto-hide / return** (ds-06 §2; adab voice gate): a small `chromeVisible` boolean — either local ephemeral UI state in this widget or a field on the E13-T02 reader-state store (display-only; mutates no engine state, writes no `review_log`). A tap on the page interior toggles the transient controls' visibility; when shown, an auto-hide `Timer` (e.g. ~3 s, a const in this file) fades them back out. The transition is a single `AnimatedOpacity`/`FadeTransition` at `motion.duration.short` with `motion.curve.standard` — **no** slide-up dashboard, scale-bounce, glow, or staggered "reveal"; the chrome appears and disappears the way a calm reading surface does. Respect `MediaQuery.disableAnimations`/reduce-motion by snapping visibility without the fade. The timer/controller is disposed in `dispose()`.

4. **The riwāyah is the one element that does not fully hide** (R2; ds-13 §5; E13-T07): per "always name the riwāyah, never present the muṣḥaf as 'the Quran' absolutely", the `displayName`/`riwayah` label stays present (it may dim, never disappear) while the transient zoom/theme/jump controls auto-hide — so at every moment the named edition is on screen. Encode this as an explicit branch (the riwāyah band is outside the `AnimatedOpacity` that wraps the transient controls), not an accident of timing.

5. **No-dashboard enforcement (the heart of this task)** (ds-13 §3/§4; mushaf-integrity rule 10; adab R1/R3): the chrome introduces, and the reader as a whole carries, **none** of — a badge, counter, "pages read today/streak", confetti/celebration on reaching the last page or finishing a juz, a glow/halo/pulse on the current āyah, an ornamental/gilt border or drop-shadow drawn over the glyph layer, a page-flip sound effect, a haptic celebration/fanfare on page turn, a progress ring/score/scoreboard, raw D/S/R or a retention percentage on the page, or any "this page is done/mastered/safe to drop" mark. The only marks permitted over the glyph layer are E05's diagnostic coordinate overlays (weak-line, mutashābihāt-anchor) toggled in E13-T05 — diagnostic, never congratulatory. This is asserted by the presence-guard test (Tests §1) and by the adab `template.md` conscience pass recorded on the PR.

6. **No piety/wuḍūʾ/ritual gate** (ds-13 §2; mushaf-integrity rule 10): nothing the chrome adds gates opening the muṣḥaf or paging behind a wuḍūʾ prompt, a piety pledge, an intention checkbox, or any ritual checkpoint — the app has no authority to impose ritual conditions. If an *optional* reverent opening framing (a quiet taʿawwudh/basmalah line) is ever shown, it is dismissible, non-blocking, never mandatory, and never guilt-laden; this task ships **no** such gate and asserts the page is reachable with zero ritual interstitial.

7. **Pitfalls to avoid**: drawing the controls as a translucent panel/scrim **over** the page interior instead of thin edge bands (covers the words); a slide-up "toolbar"/bottom-sheet dashboard, scale-bounce, glow, or staggered reveal instead of a plain `motion.duration.short` fade (celebratory motion — ds-06 §2); letting the riwāyah label auto-hide with the transient controls (breaks R2 "always named"); adding a "pages read"/streak counter, a percentage, or a D/S/R readout "to be helpful" (gamification + raw-state leak — C-031, ds-13 §4); a confetti/haptic/sound flourish on the last page or on clearing a jump (no celebration tier exists — ds-06 §2); a `HapticFeedback`/`SystemSound` call on page turn (the pager's slide is the whole affordance — E13-T03); a mandatory taʿawwudh/wuḍūʾ interstitial before the page (device-gating — ds-13 §2); using physical `left`/`right` insets instead of logical `start`/`end` (RTL breakage); reaching into E05's glyph layer or overlay painter to "decorate" it; ignoring `disableAnimations`/reduce-motion; a `DateTime.now()` anywhere (use the auto-hide `Timer` for the delay, never a clock read for state).

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] The controls are laid out as thin `start`/`end`/top/bottom edge bands using logical insets (`EdgeInsetsDirectional`/`PositionedDirectional`); nothing the chrome draws covers, frames, tints, glows behind, or decorates the page interior/glyph layer; only design-token **names** are referenced (no raw color/size literals).
- [ ] Transient controls (zoom, theme, jump-to/page affordances) auto-hide on a calm timer and on a page tap, and return on a tap, via a single `motion.duration.short` fade — no slide-up dashboard, scale-bounce, glow, or staggered reveal; reduce-motion (`disableAnimations`) snaps visibility without the fade; any timer/controller is disposed.
- [ ] The riwāyah/edition `displayName` label (E13-T07) remains present at all times (it may dim, never fully disappear) while the transient controls hide — so the named edition is always on screen (R2); this is an explicit branch, not a timing accident.
- [ ] The reader surface carries **none** of: a badge, counter, "pages read"/streak, confetti/celebration, glow/pulse on an āyah, ornamental/gilt border or drop-shadow over the glyph layer, page-flip sound, haptic fanfare on page turn, progress ring/score/scoreboard, raw D/S/R or percentage on the page, or any "done/mastered/safe to drop" mark — asserted by the presence-guard test.
- [ ] No wuḍūʾ/piety/intention/ritual gate or mandatory interstitial guards opening the muṣḥaf or paging; the page is reachable with zero ritual checkpoint; any reverent opening framing (if present) is optional, dismissible, and non-blocking.
- [ ] The chrome mutates no engine state — no `review_log` write, no `Card` mutation, no `due_at` re-derivation; `chromeVisible` is display-only reader state.
- [ ] Every show/hide and edge control carries a localized `Semantics` label (keys referenced; values land in E13-T09); the chrome opens no socket and fetches nothing; every new Dart file carries the REUSE SPDX header (`GPL-3.0-or-later`) and a `///` on each public declaration, and passes the analyzer/lint config and `dart format`.

## Tests

Dart tests run in CI on every PR (`flutter test`); any test that renders the page loads the **real KFGQPC page fonts** via `FontLoader` (never Ahem) under an RTL `Directionality`. **Write the presence-guard and auto-hide tests first.**

- `features/test/mushaf/mushaf_chrome_no_dashboard_test.dart` — **presence guard** (widget): pump `MushafChrome` over a fake reader-state store and a stub/real page; assert by widget-tree search that **no** banned surface is present — no `ConfettiWidget`/celebration widget, no streak/counter/"pages read" `Text`, no D/S/R or `%` readout in any reader `Text`, no progress ring/score, no `BoxShadow`/gilt border/glow `DecoratedBox` over the glyph area, no page-flip `AudioPlayer`/`SystemSound`, no `HapticFeedback` on page change. A grep over `mushaf_chrome.dart` (and a tree assertion) finds none of `Confetti`, `streak`, `badge`, `score`, `HapticFeedback`, `SystemSound`, `AudioPlayer`, or a `%`/D/S/R format on the page. Drive the pager to the **last** page and assert no celebration/haptic/sound fires.
- `features/test/mushaf/mushaf_chrome_autohide_test.dart` — **auto-hide / return** (widget, fake clock for the timer): assert the transient controls (zoom/theme/jump) are visible after a page tap and fade out after the auto-hide delay elapses (`pump` past the timer), and return on a subsequent tap; assert the transition is a single fade (`AnimatedOpacity`/`FadeTransition`), not a slide/scale/glow; under `MediaQuery(disableAnimations: true)` assert visibility snaps with no fade. Assert the auto-hide uses the in-widget `Timer`, not `DateTime.now()`.
- `features/test/mushaf/mushaf_chrome_riwayah_persists_test.dart` — **riwāyah always named** (widget): after the transient controls auto-hide, assert the riwāyah/`displayName` label (E13-T07) is still present in the tree (R2) while the zoom/theme controls are gone; assert the riwāyah band is outside the transient `AnimatedOpacity`.
- `features/test/mushaf/mushaf_chrome_edges_rtl_test.dart` — **edge layout + RTL** (widget): under `Locale('ar')`/`Locale('fa')`/`Locale('ckb')` assert the control bands use logical `start`/`end` insets (no physical `left`/`right`), sit at the edges (not over the page interior), and that the page (base layer) fills the surface; assert each interactive control exposes a non-empty `Semantics` label and meets the `touch.min` floor.
- `features/test/mushaf/mushaf_chrome_no_gate_test.dart` — **no piety gate** (widget): pump the reader route and assert the page is reachable with **zero** ritual interstitial — no wuḍūʾ/piety/intention dialog or blocking overlay appears before the page; if an optional opening framing renders, assert it is dismissible and non-blocking (the page is interactive without dismissing it).
- **Offline / no-network + no-mutation guard** (eng-write-dart-test): a throwing `HttpOverrides` is installed via the shared test bootstrap for every test above (C-048); a banned-import grep over `mushaf_chrome.dart` finds no `http`/`dio`/`HttpClient`/`drift`/DAO symbol, no engine-mutation symbol, no audio/mic symbol, and no `DateTime.now()`/`Calendar.current`; the fake store records zero engine writes from any chrome interaction. (The full real-font theme/zoom/overlay golden matrix is E13-T10; this task supplies the chrome-visible/-hidden and no-dashboard guards.)
