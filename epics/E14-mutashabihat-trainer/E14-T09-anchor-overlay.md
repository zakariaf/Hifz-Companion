## Goal

The discrimination drill's anchor hint exists as a **coordinate `Rect` overlay on the immutable glyph layer** — never a re-typeset. Given a `MutashabihMemberView`'s `distinguishingWordIndices` (the ayah-relative word indices E14-T01 validated and E14-T06 deserialized) and the **same** bundled QUL word geometry the glyphs are laid out from, a pure resolver maps each index to the page's `(lineNumber, position)` `WordRef`s, and E14-T08's drill feeds them to E05's `MushafOverlayPainter` as a `mutashabihAnchor` `OverlayMarker` drawn over — never into — the unmodified KFGQPC glyph page. After E14-T08 reveals a branch, the anchor highlight appears (calm, `motion.duration.short`, Reduce-Motion-respecting); the sacred text is rendered, not edited, reshaped, reflowed, or reconstructed to produce it. This task owns the **index→WordRef resolver + the anchor marker it hands the painter**; the painter contract, the page view, the reveal choreography, and the drill route are E05/E14-T08.

## Implementation notes

**TEST-FIRST (correctness-critical):** the anchor sits on the sacred surface — a wrong box implies a wrong word. Write the resolver unit suite in `## Tests` below **before** the resolver body; the ayah-relative→page-relative mapping cases, the cross-line word case, the multi-word case, and the fail-loud (empty/missing geometry) case must exist and **fail** before the resolver is implemented.

1. **A pure resolver, in `quran` (or `engine`-pure), not in the widget.** Add `anchorWordRefs(MutashabihMemberView member, PageGeometry geometry) → List<WordRef>` as a **total, side-effect-free** function alongside the E05 layout/geometry types (in the `quran` package that owns `PageGeometry`/`WordRef`/`assemblePage`, or a pure helper in `engine` if the geometry types are reachable there — never inside `features`). It reads geometry only; it touches no Drift, no `DateTime.now()`, no IO, no network. The drill `View`/controller calls it and hands the result to the painter — the resolver itself is testable without a widget.

2. **Map ayah-relative word index → page-relative `WordRef`.** `distinguishingWordIndices` are indices into the **member ayah's** word sequence (E14-T01 validated them against that ayah's word count). The bundled QUL geometry carries, per word, its `ayahId` (or sūra:ayah ref), its `page_number`, `line_number`, and `position` on the page. The resolver: for the member's `ayahId`, take that ayah's words **in their stored order** from the geometry, index into them by each `distinguishingWordIndices` entry, and emit the resolved word's `WordRef(lineNumber, position)`. The result is a `List<WordRef>` (one or more words; the diverging phrase may be multi-word). **A single ayah can straddle two muṣḥaf lines**, so two indices of the same ayah may resolve to different `lineNumber`s — handle that, never assume one line per ayah.

3. **Build exactly one `mutashabihAnchor` marker, never N markers of N kinds.** The output the drill mounts is one `OverlayMarker(kind: OverlayKind.mutashabihAnchor, pageNumber: <the member's page>, words: anchorWordRefs(...))`. All resolved words for a member ride **one** marker so the painter draws them as one calm highlight group; do not invent a new `OverlayKind`, do not reuse `weakLine`/`errorPosition`/`currentAyah`. The painter (E05) owns the box geometry, the rounded-rect, the calm token colour, and the Reduce-Motion-safe paint — T09 hands it coordinates only.

4. **Never reconstruct, re-typeset, or persist text.** The resolver consumes the **same** `PageGeometry` the glyph page is assembled from and emits only `(line, position)` refs — it never reads the Tanzil text, never builds a `TextSpan`, never re-renders the phrase in another font/widget, never normalizes/searches/logs glyph codes, and never persists a rebuilt verse. The highlight is drawn *over* the unmodified glyph layer in place (engineering 08 §4 "we refuse to re-typeset a highlighted phrase"; science 05 §6 anti-pattern). There is no shaped Arabic on this path to measure — boxes come from bundled geometry, not from `TextPainter`.

5. **Fail loudly on a missing/ambiguous geometry mapping; never guess a box.** If the member's `ayahId` is not present in the page geometry handed in, or an index resolves to no word (a dataset/geometry skew T01's load-time range check could not catch against *live* geometry), the resolver surfaces it as a typed, total failure (a sealed result / empty-with-diagnostic the drill can render as "no anchor available", **never** a silently dropped or guessed rectangle, and never tofu over the wrong word). The sacred surface fails visibly, per domain-mushaf-text-integrity rule 8. No `try?`-style swallow.

6. **The page the anchor draws on is the member's own page — never a re-paged or re-flowed surface.** The resolver's `pageNumber` is the member ayah's bundled page; the drill composes E05's `MushafPageView` for that page (its dedicated `qpcFontFamily(pageNumber)`, `fontFamilyFallback: const []`), and the overlay painter sits as the sibling layer at the **same** scale/geometry. Zoom/sepia/dark (E05 §5) transform the glyph+overlay stack uniformly; the anchor box scales **with** the page, never reflows it. T09 adds no paging, no font selection, no theme logic — it only produces the refs for the page T08 is already showing.

7. **Accessibility — not colour alone (delegated to the painter, asserted here).** The anchor must be perceivable without colour (a calm outline/box shape carries it, per ui-mushaf-page-view), and its presence is exposed to the drill's `Semantics` (E14-T08/E08 own the screen-reader label "distinguishing word(s) highlighted" in fa/ckb/ar). T09 ensures the marker it emits is the shape-bearing `mutashabihAnchor` kind (so the painter draws the box, not a colour wash) and that the resolved `WordRef` set is correct so the label maps to the right words.

8. **Pitfalls to avoid:** resolving against **ayah** order on the page when the index is meant to be ayah-relative word order (or vice-versa) — pin the convention with the cross-line test; assuming one `lineNumber` per ayah (a straddling ayah breaks it); building a marker per word instead of one grouped marker; introducing a new `OverlayKind` or re-typesetting the phrase in a second widget; reading the Tanzil text or any glyph string to "find" the word (search the *geometry*, never the text); measuring shaped Arabic / calling `TextPainter` for a box; guessing or dropping a box on a missing mapping instead of failing loudly; putting the resolver in `features` or making it read Drift/`DateTime.now()`; encoding the anchor by colour alone; any celebratory motion/flash on the highlight; persisting a reconstructed verse for the marker.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] A pure, total `anchorWordRefs(MutashabihMemberView, PageGeometry) → List<WordRef>` (plus its `mutashabihAnchor` `OverlayMarker` builder) exists in the `quran`/`engine`-pure layer — **not** in `features`; it reads only the bundled geometry, touches no Drift, no IO, no network, and no `DateTime.now()`.
- [ ] Each `distinguishingWordIndices` entry (ayah-relative) resolves to the correct page-relative `WordRef(lineNumber, position)` via the bundled QUL geometry; a multi-word divergence yields multiple refs; a single ayah straddling two lines yields refs on different `lineNumber`s.
- [ ] The resolver output is composed into **exactly one** `OverlayMarker(kind: mutashabihAnchor, pageNumber: <member page>, words: …)`; no new `OverlayKind`, no per-word markers, no reuse of `weakLine`/`errorPosition`/`currentAyah`.
- [ ] No Quran text is read, reconstructed, re-typeset, normalized, searched, logged, or persisted to produce the anchor; the resolver consumes geometry only and emits `(line, position)` refs; glyph codes stay opaque; no `TextPainter`/shaped-Arabic measurement on this path.
- [ ] A missing/ambiguous geometry mapping (unknown `ayahId`, index resolving to no word) surfaces as a typed, total failure the drill can render as "no anchor available" — never a silently dropped, guessed, or mis-placed box; no `try?`-style swallow.
- [ ] The anchor draws over E05's immutable glyph page for the **member's own page** at the same scale/geometry; the page is never reflowed, re-paged, font-swapped, or re-typeset to show the highlight; zoom/sepia/dark transform the glyph+overlay stack uniformly.
- [ ] The anchor is the shape-bearing `mutashabihAnchor` marker (box, not colour alone); marker colour/radius/motion are E05 design-system tokens referenced by name — no inlined hex/radius/ms in this task.
- [ ] The resolver surfaces no on-screen number and no methodology claim; the anchor's behaviour implements the already-graded CLAIMS rows C-028/C-029 (rendered in E19); **no** CLAIMS id is invented.
- [ ] Every `public` declaration carries a `///` doc comment; the file carries the REUSE SPDX header (`GPL-3.0-or-later`) and passes the analyzer/lint config and `dart format`.

## Tests

`packages/quran/test/mutashabihat/anchor_word_refs_test.dart` (mirrors the source; pure `package:test`/`flutter_test` unit, runs in the fast CI job under the throwing `HttpOverrides` offline guard — the resolver opens no socket). Fixtures are **explicit `PageGeometry` literals** built inline with known word boxes — no Drift, no asset IO, no hidden clock. Required cases, written **FIRST**:

- **Single-word anchor (happy path):** a member ayah with a known word layout + `distinguishingWordIndices: [k]` resolves to the exact `WordRef(lineNumber, position)` of the k-th word of that ayah in the geometry. Deep-equal the `List<WordRef>`.
- **Multi-word divergence:** `distinguishingWordIndices: [i, j]` resolves to two refs in the dataset's index order; the emitted marker carries both words.
- **Ayah straddling two lines:** an ayah whose words span `lineNumber` L and L+1; two indices on opposite sides resolve to **different** `lineNumber`s — proving the resolver never assumes one line per ayah.
- **One grouped marker:** the builder yields exactly one `OverlayMarker` of `kind: mutashabihAnchor` with `pageNumber` = the member's page and all resolved words; not N markers, not a foreign kind.
- **Fail-loud on missing mapping:** a member whose `ayahId` is absent from the supplied geometry, and an index that resolves to no word, each yield the typed failure / empty-with-diagnostic result — **not** a thrown-and-swallowed error, a guessed box, or a silently empty success masquerading as "no anchor".
- **No-text invariant (structural):** assert (by type/grep in the suite or a compile-time boundary) that the resolver returns only `WordRef`/`OverlayMarker` and never a `String`/`TextSpan`/glyph-code/verse — it cannot reconstruct text.

`packages/quran/test/mutashabihat/anchor_marker_golden_test.dart` (vector/value golden, optional companion): a frozen `(member, geometry) → List<WordRef>` row asserted exactly, pinning the index→ref mapping against regression.
