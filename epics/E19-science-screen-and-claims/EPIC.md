# E19 — Science Screen & Claims

The offline "The science we follow" screen, and the bundled CLAIMS register it renders. Each registered claim becomes one calm source row — a plain-language headline in the ḥāfiẓ's own frame (why revision is spaced, why whole pages, why no streaks, why nothing is ever "safe to drop", that the schedule is your own grades, that there is no AI), a neutral certainty label describing the *strength of the evidence*, and a named, dated, on-device source that is tappable but never depends on the network. It is a read-only projection of `docs/science/CLAIMS.md`: no claim ships unsourced, every `[TRAD]` row names its source and issues no fiqh ruling, and the whole surface travels into airplane mode with the app.

## Why this epic exists

The PRD's whole position — *the app that makes sure a ḥāfiẓ never silently loses the Quran* (PRD §2) — only earns the trust of huffaz, teachers, and scholars if the app can *show its work*. This audience parses privacy and authority critically and has been burned by the category (the Muslim Pro location-broker breach; [10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md](../../docs/design-system/10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md) §1), so a scheduling rule a ḥāfiẓ is asked to follow daily — "this page is due again within your cycle," "reciting from memory beats re-reading," "we never declare a page safe to drop" — cannot be the app's bare opinion. The science foundation enforces a one-directional, load-bearing chain that *ends on this screen*: *verified primary source → graded claim in a synthesis doc → `CLAIMS.md` row → engine rule + on-screen copy → the science screen* ([11-the-in-app-science-screen.md](../../docs/science/11-the-in-app-science-screen.md) §1; [CLAIMS.md](../../docs/science/CLAIMS.md) intro). The absolute rule is that if a statement is not registered with at least one verified source and an evidence grade, the app must not state it — so a claim with no row is a release-blocking defect, not a copy nit.

This screen is also where three non-negotiables become *visible* rather than merely true. **No-AI / offline (PRD C1, C2):** because the app runs no model and makes no live call, the screen must be a static, bundled, read-only view — every word pre-written, reviewed, and shipped inside the verified core pack, useful in airplane mode the same as the muṣḥaf (11 §2). **Honesty about certainty (11 §5):** the grade describes the strength of the *evidence*, kept strictly separate from any promise about *this ḥāfiẓ's* Quran — near-100% retention comes from the cycle ceiling and overlearning, never a probability number, and a high grade is never a guarantee that a page is "safe." **Sect-neutral adab (PRD §4, R2, R6):** the methodology and `[TRAD]` rows (the decay ḥadīth, the seven manāzil, the ʿarḍa, talaqqī) are surfaced as named, dated scholarship — collection and number with grading — framed as methodology, issuing no fiqh ruling, madhhab- and sect-neutral, a servant to the teacher and the *sanad*, flagging "needs scholarly review" where sign-off is pending. Building the register-as-data, its faithful renderer, and the grade-coverage gate together — instead of hand-writing science into feature strings — is the cheapest tier at which "no claim unsourced" and "no promise of certainty" become build-or-fail checks rather than review-time hopes.

## Scope

### In scope

- **The CLAIMS register as bundled, offline data** — a typed, read-only representation of the `docs/science/CLAIMS.md` rows (grouped A–J: memory & forgetting · spacing · engine math · retrieval · interference · serial recall · overlearning · methodology · motivation · cross-cutting honesty), each row carrying its `C-NNN` id, plain claim headline, the value/rule the engine uses, the named source(s) (author/year/venue, or hadith collection + number + grading), the sealed `EvidenceGrade`, the app surface, and the caveat — shipped inside the verified core pack / app binary, loaded once, never fetched.
- **A grade-coverage / no-orphan-claim gate** — a release-blocking check that every claim id rendered on the screen resolves to a register row with ≥1 source and a valid grade, that no grade is unknown, and (as far as it can be machine-checked) that no user-facing factual string elsewhere asserts a fact absent from the register.
- **The "The science we follow" screen** — a calm, scannable, read-only surface that groups the claim rows by theme (11 §3) into short cards, mounts as a reference screen reachable from Settings/About, opens when wanted, and carries no engagement mechanics (no "N of M cards read", no badge, no progress bar).
- **The science source row** — the atomic two-layer row rendering exactly one register entry: a plain headline (`type.body`, the ḥāfiẓ's frame — *murājaʿa*, pages, juz, cycle) on the face; the named source, the certainty label, and any caveat in an optional "the evidence" expansion; with an optional tappable URL that visibly *leaves the app*, opens the system browser, and degrades harmlessly offline (`ui-science-source-row`).
- **The certainty label, composed into each row** — the neutral evidence-certainty badge built in E10 (`ui-certainty-label`), mapping the sealed `EvidenceGrade` to plain confidence-about-the-evidence words plus a non-color glyph, never a star rating, "proven" badge, traffic-light color, or retention percentage — plus the always-reachable plain-words grade legend.
- **The `[TRAD]` / methodology framing** — religious and methodology rows (the decay ḥadīth *Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 5032*; the seven manāzil; the ʿarḍa *Bukhārī 4998*; talaqqī / sanad) shown with collection + number + grading, as methodology, sect-neutral, servant-to-the-teacher, issuing no ruling, with "needs scholarly review" shown plainly where pending.
- **The honesty / non-coercion surfaces** — the "no retention-percentage promise" framing (near-100% via the cycle ceiling + overlearning, C-016/C-017/C-025), the calm presentation of decay and the decay ḥadīth as the honest premise of loss-prevention (never a threat), and the registered no-streaks/no-guilt claim (C-041…C-045) shown as a visible trust signal.
- **Full fa/ckb/ar localization, RTL-native** — every headline, source attribution, certainty phrase, legend entry, "needs scholarly review" note, and "opens in your browser" hint as transcreated (not literally translated) ARB strings; locale numerals (Extended Arabic-Indic for fa/ckb, Arabic-Indic for ar) on every year/number via the shared numeral path; FSI/PDI bidi isolation on Latin author/venue/URL runs.
- **The screen's wiring** — the `science` feature module (dumb View + 1:1 ViewModel) and the Riverpod read model exposing the bundled register rows to the screen, mounted in the existing shell behind Settings/About.
- **Accessibility + offline + grade-coverage tests** — per-locale (fa/ckb/ar) widget/golden coverage on the real bundled UI fonts, a grayscale/deuteranope check that the grade survives without color, an `HttpOverrides` offline guard asserting tapping a source link triggers no in-app fetch, and the test-first grade-coverage / no-orphan-claim gate.

### Out of scope

- **Authoring or editing the science itself** — adding/regrading/sourcing a `CLAIMS.md` row, the synthesis docs, or `REFERENCES.md` is the science foundation's work governed by **domain-claims-register-and-science-screen**; this epic *renders* the already-authored register and must not invent a claim. (Doc edits that the screen reveals are needed are raised against the foundation, not written here.)
- **The `EvidenceGrade` enum, the pure `certaintyLabel(EvidenceGrade)` mapping, the neutral badge widget, and the plain-words legend leaf** → built in **E10 mihrab-component-library** (E10-T06, `ui-certainty-label`); this epic *composes* them into the row and screen.
- **The engine math each claim describes** — the trust clamp `due = min(ideal, ceiling)`, the cycle ceiling, FSRS D/S/R, "never safe to drop", the cold-start seeds, the load balancer → **E04 scheduling-engine** (governed by **domain-scheduling-engine-rules**); the screen reads the registered *description*, it never re-derives the rule.
- **The verified core-pack download + SHA-256 fail-closed verifier the bundled science data ships inside** → **E05 quran-data-and-rendering** (governed by **domain-asset-pack-integrity**); this epic relies on the verified pack, it never opens a socket.
- **The privacy/About panel, the "what stays / what we send / what we can see" rows, the airplane-mode proof, and the repo/asset-URL verification links** that sit beside this screen → **E16 settings-profiles-teacher** (from [10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md](../../docs/design-system/10-privacy-and-trust-ux.md)); this epic adds the science screen reachable from the same Settings/About area.
- **The recite/grade flow, heat-map, notifications, onboarding, and Today copy** whose user-facing claims also trace to the register → owned by **E11/E12/E15/E18**; this epic owns only the science *screen* and the register-as-data contract, not those features' strings.
- **The CI no-network / banned-import / dependency-allow-list gate plumbing** → authored in **E01 repo-scaffold-and-ci**; this epic adds the grade-coverage gate and must give the existing gates nothing to catch.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] **Renderer, never author**: every fact on the screen renders one `C-NNN` register row; no science claim, citation, or grade is hand-written into a row, the screen, or a feature string; the grade-coverage gate proves no orphan fact ships and no rendered claim lacks a source or a known grade.
- [ ] **Offline / no-network / no-AI by construction**: the register data, citations, grades, and confidence phrases ship inside the binary / verified core pack and render locally; no runtime fetch of claims or "latest research", no model generates any wording; the widget-test bootstrap installs a throwing `HttpOverrides`, a link tap triggers no in-app fetch, and the banned-import gate stays green over the new code.
- [ ] **No promise of certainty**: no retention percentage appears anywhere; near-100% is attributed to the cycle ceiling + overlearning; a grade is rendered as confidence about the *evidence* (text tag + non-color glyph), never as a star rating, "proven" badge, percentage, or "safe to drop" / "mastered" state.
- [ ] **Text fidelity**: the screen renders no Quran glyph and re-typesets no āyah — a `[TRAD]` row names a hadith by collection + number as on-device text, never as rendered muṣḥaf; goldens use the real bundled UI fonts, never `Ahem`, never the QPC glyph fonts.
- [ ] **Sect-neutral adab**: every `[TRAD]` / methodology row issues no fiqh ruling, names its source (collection + number + grading), stays madhhab/sect-neutral, is framed servant-to-the-teacher, shows "needs scholarly review" where pending, and is not ranked above the empirical grades as more authoritative; the decay ḥadīth and forgetting curve are framed calmly, never as a threat — all passing the **domain-adab-and-religious-integrity** conscience-check.
- [ ] **No gamification of worship**: no streak, badge, progress bar, "N of M cards read", celebratory motion, or guilt/loss/fear copy anywhere on the science surface; the screen is reference, opened when wanted (PRD R3, C6).
- [ ] **RTL + fa/ckb/ar localization**: every user-facing string is an `AppLocalizations` key (ar template + fa/ckb transcreation), no hardcoded literal; every year/number renders through `numberFormatFor(locale)` in the locale digit set; Latin author/venue/URL runs are FSI/PDI bidi-isolated; the per-locale golden matrix is green and the ckb transcreation reflows without clipping at 200% text scale.
- [ ] **Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA)**: text ≥4.5:1 and non-text ≥3:1 across light/sepia/dark; the grade is conveyed by its text tag + non-color glyph, never color alone; the row reflows at 200% with no truncation or horizontal scroll; each row is `MergeSemantics`-merged into one localized phrase (headline + source + confidence label + "external link"), decoration `ExcludeSemantics`; a link affordance is ≥48×48dp — all asserted by `meetsGuideline` and a grayscale/deuteranope check.
- [ ] **On-screen wording matches the engine**: each row's copy matches the value/rule the engine actually uses for that claim — no drift between what the app *says* and what it *does*.
- [ ] **Tests**: the register parser, the grade-coverage / no-orphan-claim gate, and the certainty-row composition are unit-tested test-first; the row and screen have per-locale goldens on real fonts and the offline link guard; the suite runs in CI on every PR.

## Risks

- **A claim is hand-written into a row with no register entry.** The single most damaging failure: an unsourced or ungraded fact reaching a ḥāfiẓ. *Mitigation:* the screen reads only from the bundled register (T01/T03), authoring lives in **domain-claims-register-and-science-screen**, and the T02 grade-coverage gate fails the build on any rendered claim id that lacks a row, a source, or a known grade — it is a CI gate, not a review hope.
- **The grade is rendered as a guarantee about the user's Quran.** A "★★★★★", a "proven" badge, or a retention percentage would collapse "strong evidence for spacing" into "this page is safe" — a forbidden promise (11 §5; PRD §7.12). *Mitigation:* the grade is composed only via the E10 `CertaintyLabel` (confidence-about-the-evidence words + non-color glyph); a neutral-styling golden and a grayscale check assert no star/color/percentage; the no-promise framing is its own registered surface (T08).
- **A `[TRAD]` row drifts into a ruling or breaks sect-neutrality.** Surfacing a hadith or methodology could be misread as a fiqh verdict or a madhhab stance. *Mitigation:* the T05 variant shows collection + number + grading as *methodology only*, sect-neutral, servant-to-the-teacher, with "needs scholarly review" where pending; every `[TRAD]` string passes the **domain-adab-and-religious-integrity** conscience-check; no row interprets a verse or ranks `[TRAD]` above empirical grades.
- **A source link makes the screen depend on the network.** A citation hidden behind a link that is meaningless offline would break the offline guarantee and the on-device trust. *Mitigation:* the full reference is always on-device text; the URL is an optional convenience that visibly leaves the app and simply does nothing offline (T06), proven by an `HttpOverrides` guard that a tap triggers no in-app fetch.
- **The screen acquires engagement mechanics.** A "you've read 5 of 12 science cards" bar or a finish badge would turn reference into a manufactured return — a gamification breach (PRD R3/C6; 11 §6). *Mitigation:* the screen is reference, opened when wanted; no counter, badge, streak, or progress bar is built, and the no-coercion rule is a DoD line and an adab-check item.
- **Goldens pass in one locale and rot in the others.** A row that reads in `ar` may clip in `ckb` (the longest transcreation) or mis-order a Latin author run in a mixed RTL line. *Mitigation:* every golden is a fa × ckb × ar matrix on the real bundled fonts at 1× and 200%; the Latin author/venue/URL runs are FSI/PDI-isolated and years use the shared locale-numeral path so a bidi/numeral fix lands once.
- **The register-as-data drifts from the muṣḥaf/doc source of truth.** A row bundled in the app could fall behind a corrected `CLAIMS.md`. *Mitigation:* the bundled register ships inside the *versioned, checksum-verified* core pack (E05); corrections ship as an app/pack update, never a silent remote edit; the loader rejects an unknown grade as a data defect rather than rendering a fallback.
