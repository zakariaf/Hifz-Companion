## Goal

Tapping a page-card opens a **full-screen recite/grade route** that masks the immutable E13 reader glyph page and lets the ·∏•ƒÅfi·∫ì recite from memory, **reveal line-by-line on tap**, **mark stumble lines** by tapping ‚â•48dp grown hit-areas that draw a **coordinate overlay on the immutable glyph layer** (never a re-typeset), and grade the page on a **four-button band that is disabled until ‚â•1 reveal** (reading as *waiting*, not broken). ## Implementation notes

This route is **layout/interaction**, not engine math ‚Äî there is **no** frozen-vector or `glados` work here (that lives in E12-T06 and E04). But two pieces are correctness-sensitive and are written **test-first** (see Tests): the **disabled-until-revealed band gate** (a premature grade is a sacred-text-grade error this screen must structurally prevent) and the **undo reversal** (a fat-fingered sacred-text grade must be recoverable). The stumble **overlay-not-reflow** invariant is proven by golden.

1. **Where it lives.** The route is a leaf under a `recite` feature: `packages/features/lib/src/recite/recite_grade_screen.dart` (the dumb full-screen `ConsumerWidget`), `packages/features/lib/src/recite/recite_view_model.dart` (the 1:1 view-model), `packages/features/lib/src/recite/recite_providers.dart` (scoped providers), and `widgets/` leaves: `recite_surface.dart` (the masked glyph page + reveal + stumble hit-areas), `grade_band.dart` (the four-button band, assembling the E10 leaf), `stumble_overlay.dart` (the `CustomPaint` coordinate overlay). One primary public type per file, `lower_snake_case` filenames (eng-add-feature-module).

2. **The route + the one permitted hero.** Register `page/:pageId` as a typed `GoRoute` under the E07 `ShellRoute`; T03 navigates to it with `item.pageId`. The page‚Üírecite transition is the **single** place `motion.curve.emphasized` with one `long` duration is permitted (a navigational hero, never a reward) ‚Äî referenced by name from `MotionTokens`, never an inline `Duration`/`Curve`. Under reduce-motion it collapses to a plain push (06 ¬ß5; E08 helper). Advancing to the **next** page after a grade is a calm `short`/`medium`, `standard`-eased push ‚Äî the same quiet path for every verdict.

3. **The view-model + the stage machine, no math.** `ReciteState` is immutable (`stage`, `revealedLineCount`, `stumbleLines: Set<int>` of 1-based indices, `teacherPresent`, `missedOrAlteredWord`); `hasRevealed => revealedLineCount > 0` gates the band. The view-model is an `AutoDisposeAsyncNotifier.family` keyed by `PageId` (eng-create-riverpod-store ¬ß5) exposing `revealNextLine()`, `toggleStumbleLine(int)`, `setTeacherPresent(bool)`, `submitGrade(Grade)`, `undoLastGrade()`. `submitGrade` hands `(grade, errorLines, source, missedOrAlteredWord)` to the **E12-T06 pipeline**, which persists through the single write path **before** republishing, then advances; it returns an undo handle. The view-model does **no** stability math, **never** caps a grade, and **never** reads `DateTime.now()` (the injected `CalendarDate` is the pipeline's; this layer passes no clock).

4. **Compose the immutable glyph page ‚Äî never render it (test-first invariant).** `ReciteSurface` composes the **E13** per-page glyph widget for `pageId` and masks/reveals its *surface*. It **never** builds a glyph, never re-typesets, never reflows. Lines `>= revealedLineCount` sit under an opaque mask; a tap on the surface reveals the **next** line (`revealNextLine()`), fading in at `motion.duration.short` / `motion.curve.standard` (instant under reduce-motion). The E13 page art is identical masked or revealed ‚Äî only the mask layer changes (proven by the masked-vs-revealed golden: the glyph pixels under the mask are byte-identical to the reader's).

5. **Stumble taps: a ‚â•48dp grown hit-area drawing a coordinate overlay.** For each **revealed** line index `i`, lay a transparent `Semantics` hit-area of height ‚â• `touch.min` (‚â•48dp via `SpaceTokens`/`touch.min`, **not** the visual glyph height ‚Äî pad with transparent space so the target grows without touching the glyph layer), `behavior: HitTestBehavior.translucent`. A tap calls `toggleStumbleLine(i + 1)` and fires `HapticFeedback.selectionClick()` (`haptic.selection` ‚Äî a discrete, reversible choice; 06 ¬ß4). The marked line is drawn by `StumbleOverlay`, a `CustomPaint` that paints a quiet highlight **on top of** the E13 page rect using the reader's per-line coordinate rects ‚Äî an overlay of coordinates, **never** a re-layout (07 ¬ß5, R1). The overlay reads the line rects from E13; it never derives them by measuring text.

6. **`missedOrAlteredWord` is raised here, capped in the pipeline.** Marking a stumble line raises `missedOrAlteredWord` on the state; the screen passes the flag to E12-T06, which caps the normalized grade at `Grade.hard` **before** the `ReviewInput` is emitted. This widget **never** caps the grade itself and **never** emits a final grade ‚Äî it reports the user's taps. The band may *suggest* a grade from the stumble count (a default selection), but the grade stays user-confirmable (07 ¬ß5; C-033).

7. **The four-button band, disabled until ‚â•1 reveal (test-first gate).** Assemble the **E10** grade-band leaf: four `FilledButton`s ‚Äî Again / Hard / Good / Easy ‚Äî ‚â•56dp tall, ‚â•48dp wide, `space.2` apart, low in the thumb zone, in **RTL verb order** with **localized** verbs (`l10n.gradeNeededHelp` / `gradeMinorMistakes` / `gradeRecitedClean` / `gradeEffortless`, never enum names; verbs from PRD ¬ß6.3). The band is **disabled until `hasRevealed`** and reads as **waiting** ‚Äî a calm dimmed band with a quiet "reveal to grade" hint ‚Äî never a dead/broken button (07 ¬ß6; NN/g #1). Disabled/pressed/focused are M3 **state layers** over role colors with a visible focus ring (`color.outline`; WCAG 2.2 SC 2.4.7), never ad-hoc opacity. A widget test, written first, asserts every grade button is non-tappable until ‚â•1 reveal and tappable after.

8. **Commit, then undo (test-first reversal).** On commit, `submitGrade` fires `HapticFeedback.lightImpact()` (`haptic.confirm`) and a quiet advance ‚Äî **no** success haptic, **no** chime, **no** confetti, **no** streak; the path for Good/Easy is byte-identical to Again (06 ¬ß2). A brief, non-intrusive **undo** affordance (a `SnackBar` with an action, within thumb reach) lets a fat-fingered sacred-text grade be reversed; `undoLastGrade()` reverses the just-committed grade through the single write path (it appends a corrective row ‚Äî it does **not** mutate the append-only `review_log`). A widget test, written first, asserts undo reverses the committed grade and returns the route to the prior page's grading stage.

9. **The teacher toggle slot ‚Äî wire the slot, not the switch.** Lay out the E10 sign-off-toggle leaf in the band's lower region and pass `setTeacherPresent`; the **behaviour** (the source switch, the teacher-sourced marker, the halaqa path) is **E12-T08**. This route only reserves the slot, reports `source = teacher` when the toggle is on, and keeps it visually distinct so self/teacher are never conflated (07 ¬ß7). Do not author the marker or the override here.

10. **RTL by geometry; the AppBar echoes page identity.** Every inset/alignment is `EdgeInsetsDirectional`/`AlignmentDirectional`/`Positioned.directional` ‚Äî no `EdgeInsets.only(left:/right:)`, no `Alignment.centerLeft/Right` (grep-banned in `features/**`). The four verbs read right-to-left as a consequence of the locale (never a manual reversal); do **not** wrap the route in a hardcoded `Directionality`. The AppBar shows the page identity ("Page €≤€µ€≥ ¬∑ Juz €±€≥") via `numberFormatFor(locale)` injected into the ARB placeholder and bidi-isolated (FSI/PDI) so it never reorders. The calm **exit/abort** sits on the AppBar leading (start) edge, in thumb reach (07 ¬ß5). The mu·π£·∏•af glyph page, ayah-end marker, and sajda sign are **never** mirrored, flipped, or re-shaped (eng-rtl-and-bidi-layout rule 4/9).

11. **Accessibility announce path.** Each grade button's `Semantics` announces its verdict **and its consequence** ("again ‚Äî review again soon") so a non-visual user grades confidently (07 ¬ß5). The disabled band announces as **waiting** (the "reveal to grade" hint is in the semantics, not motion). Each stumble hit-area is a labelled `Semantics` toggle ("line €∑, marked stumble"). The toggle announces label + state. Reduce-motion degrades every transition (reveal fade, advance push, the hero) to a cross-fade/instant cut ‚Äî nothing is information-bearing in motion alone (06 ¬ß5; E08). Every interactive target is ‚â•48dp; the per-screen E08 audit passes.

12. **Pitfalls to avoid.** (a) Doing stability math or capping the grade in the widget (it belongs to E12-T06). (b) Rendering or re-typesetting an ƒÅyah, or deriving the stumble overlay by measuring text instead of reading E13's line rects. (c) Enabling the band before a reveal (kills retrieval practice; invites a dishonest/premature grade). (d) A `SegmentedButton` or shrunk targets instead of four ‚â•56dp `FilledButton`s. (e) A celebratory motion/haptic/chime on Good/Easy, or a different path for Easy than Again. (f) An irreversible grade, or an exit/undo out of thumb reach. (g) `EdgeInsets.only(left:/right:)` / `Alignment.centerLeft` / a hardcoded `Directionality` slipping in; mirroring the mu·π£·∏•af. (h) Reading `DateTime.now()` for the advance or any "next due" hint. (i) Authoring the teacher-source switch/marker that belongs to E12-T08. (j) A mid-recitation teleprompter (auto-revealing the next line before a tap) ‚Äî reveal is always user-driven and post-attempt (C-020).
